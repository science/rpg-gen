# Introduction {#introduction}

The story deviates from the published Paper Chase in that Rupert Merriwether has a large estate and he has invited a group of both skeptics and those curious about the occult to stay at his property for 5 weeks in July and August, to get away from Boston and participate in exploration of the Occult Sciences in the form of seances, mind readings, research through his massive library, and discussion among the various experts in attendance.

Some characters may know Merriwether through The Society for the Exploration of the Unexplained (SEU), and he might know others only through references from mutual acquaintances. The group arrives at various times on a Friday and is introduced to their rooms and the grounds. The 1920s opulence of the ultra rich is dramatic and disheartening. But also intoxicating up close.

I hate almost all rich people, but I think I’d be darling at it.  
\- Dorothy Parker 

The group spends a loose and informal first evening, dining on snacks provided by the staff, and wandering the grounds, perhaps encountering and engaging with other guests in an informal way. Saturday begins with a group breakfast where everyone is introduced and backgrounds given.

# The Paper Chase \- Investigation Scenes {#the-paper-chase---investigation-scenes}

## Core Investigation Philosophy {#core-investigation-philosophy}

Each investigation path should provide **tangible benefits** for the final confrontation at the church portal. Benefits include:

- **Protective measures** (salt, symbols, wards)  
- **Tactical advantages** (weapons, understanding creature's weaknesses, knowing the terrain)  
- **Strategic knowledge** (portal mechanics, Douglas's schedule, how to close/control the portal)  
- **Allies** (earning trust of NPCs who can help)

No single benefit is required, but accumulating them makes the finale more survivable and offers more options.

---

## Streamlined Locations {#streamlined-locations}

### PRIMARY INVESTIGATION SITES {#primary-investigation-sites}

1. **Library** \- Research hub, potential weapon  
2. **Ruined Church** \- Portal location, advance scouting  
3. **Lake & Grounds** \- Evidence of dimensional thinning  
4. **Garage** \- Bertrand's activities  
5. **Kitchen/Staff** \- Protective measures, intelligence  
6. **Locked Rooms** \- High-risk, high-reward intel

### REDUCED/COMBINED LOCATIONS {#reduced/combined-locations}

- Fountain, pool, summer house → **Combined into "Grounds/Water Features"**  
- Hedge maze → **Cut** (atmospheric but no benefit)  
- Gazebo → **Folded into Lake area**

# Scene 1: Arrival & Initial Exploration (Friday Evening) {#scene-1:-arrival-&-initial-exploration-(friday-evening)}

Characters arrive throughout Friday afternoon. Informal evening allows initial mingling and character introductions. The house and grounds are available for casual exploration, setting baseline familiarity.

**Purpose**: Establish setting, introduce NPCs, let players define their characters' approaches (skeptical vs. open-minded, social vs. investigative, bold vs. cautious).

---

# Scene 2: Breakfast & Free Investigation (Saturday) {#scene-2:-breakfast-&-free-investigation-(saturday)}

Formal introductions at breakfast. Rupert Merriwether explains the purpose of the gathering: to explore occult phenomena through study, discussion, and experimentation. Tonight (Saturday) will feature a seance led by Ophelia Ashcroft.

After breakfast, investigators are free to explore. Their choices determine what benefits they accumulate.

---

## INVESTIGATION PATHS & BENEFITS {#investigation-paths-&-benefits}

### PATH A: THE LIBRARY {#path-a:-the-library}

**Access**: Open to all guests  
**Time Investment**: 1-2 hours per research session

#### TIER 1: Initial Browse {#tier-1:-initial-browse}

**No roll required** \- Basic observation:

- Massive collection including rare occult texts in locked glass cases  
- Section on "dimensional theory" and "threshold phenomena" shows heavy use  
- Several books have bookmarks, margin notes in two different hands  
- Comfortable reading area with desk

**BENEFIT OPPORTUNITY**:

- **Spot Hidden**: A German Luger pistol is concealed in a desk drawer, wrapped in cloth. Someone hid this here. (5 rounds, no one will notice if taken \- **Benefit: Weapon**)

#### TIER 2: Research Session {#tier-2:-research-session}

Choose a topic and roll **Library Use**:

**"Protective Symbols & Wards"**

- **Success**: Learn that certain geometric symbols (Elder Signs, protective circles) can repel entities from beyond. Sketching these offers protection if properly drawn.  
- **BENEFIT: Knowledge of Protective Symbols** \- Can attempt to draw these at church (Hard Occult roll required, but possible. Salt is used to draw the symbols, the Occult roll becomes normal.) If successful, this creates tactical areas where the creature cannot enter without first disrupting the symbols, which takes time.

**"Dimensional Portals & Thresholds"**

- **Success**: Natural weak points exist where "layers of reality" are thin \- often near water, old structures, sites of death. Portals can be forced open through ritual but are unstable.  
- **Hard Success**: Closing a portal requires either destroying the physical anchor (object or structure) or performing a banishment ritual during specific conditions (dawn/dusk, when the creature is on one side).  
- **BENEFIT: Portal Mechanics** \- Know how portals can be closed

**"Creatures Beyond"**

- **Success**: Entities from other dimensions often have specific vulnerabilities \- some to light, some to sound, some to specific materials (silver, iron, salt).  
- **Hard Success**: Find reference to "Watchers" \- beings that exist between states and cannot fully manifest in bright light. Vulnerable when transitioning between forms.  
- **BENEFIT: Creature Weakness (Light and Lake water)** \- Understanding that bright light and water from the family lake disrupts the local entities

**"Merriwether Family History"**

- **Success**: Sarah Merriwether (Rupert's daughter) died in 1921 by drowning in the lake. Ruled accidental but investigation was cursory. She had been helping her father with research.  
- **Hard Success**: Find Rupert's private journal (poorly hidden). Recent entries discuss "Sarah's unfinished work" and "correcting my mistakes." References "the church foundation" and "father's original site."  
- **BENEFIT: Understanding Motivation** \- Rupert is trying to contact his dead daughter

#### TIER 3: Deep Research (Following up) {#tier-3:-deep-research-(following-up)}

**If investigators spend 3+ hours total in library and succeed at Hard Library Use**:

Find a slim volume: *"Rituals of Binding and Dismissal"* (Latin, translation requires Latin skill or 2 additional hours)

**BENEFIT: Binding Ritual** \- Learn a ritual that can trap an entity temporarily (15 minutes of chanting, requires 3 participants, must draw a circle). Gives the party an option to contain the creature instead of fighting.

**LIBRARY NPC ENCOUNTER \- Douglas Kimball**:

- Often in library morning and afternoon (before transformation)  
- If engaged, enthusiastically discusses "thin places" and "crossing over"  
- **Psychology**: He's obsessed but not dangerous yet \- genuinely curious  
- If befriended (Charm or shared interest), he'll mention he's been having "unusual dreams" about the church  
- **BENEFIT**: Knowing Douglas is affected helps party realize he's victim not villain

### PATH B: THE RUINED CHURCH & GRAVEYARD {#path-b:-the-ruined-church-&-graveyard}

**Access**: Open but isolated, takes 20 minutes to walk from house  
**Risk**: If visited at night, potential encounter

#### TIER 1: Exterior Investigation {#tier-1:-exterior-investigation}

**No roll required** \- Basic observation:

- Stone church from early 1700s, partially collapsed  
- Small family graveyard: Sarah Merriwether (1894-1921), several older Merriwethers  
- Fresh flowers on Sarah's grave (placed regularly)  
- Church door appears sealed but actually opens if pulled

**Spot Hidden**:

- Faint path through grass leading from church toward lake, recently used  
- Scratch marks on door frame, waist-high, as if from fingernails

**Track**:

- Muddy boot prints near door (size matches Douglas Kimball's boots)  
- Prints lead TO church, not away (he entered recently but hasn't left?)

**BENEFIT**: Understanding church is being actively used, not just ruin

#### TIER 2: Interior Investigation {#tier-2:-interior-investigation}

**Requires entering** (structural danger, potential collapse):

**Spot Hidden**:

- Floor behind altar has been disturbed \- old stones pried up and replaced  
- Candle stubs and chalk marks on floor (recent, within days)  
- Books stacked in corner: four volumes from Merriwether library, including two investigators saw in library earlier  
- **BENEFIT: Evidence of Theft** \- Someone is bringing library books here

**Occult**:

- Chalk marks are attempt at summoning circle, but badly drawn \- amateur work  
- Circle has break in it (dangerous \- no containment)  
- Church foundation alignment is off from traditional Christian east-west  
- **BENEFIT: Understanding Ritual Site** \- Knowing circle is broken means portal isn't controlled

**Structural Assessment** (Mechanical Repair or Archaeology):

- Altar is stable \- could be toppled with 3 people pushing, or 1 person with lever/crowbar  
- Collapsing altar might destroy portal anchor (if altar IS anchor)  
- **BENEFIT: Tactical Knowledge** \- Option to destroy altar in finale

#### TIER 3: Basement Discovery {#tier-3:-basement-discovery}

**Requires Hard Spot Hidden or examining floor closely after finding chalk marks**:

One stone behind the altar is actually a concealed trap door (very old). Opens to reveal steep stairs down to a small basement chamber.

**In Basement**:

- Older foundation stones with pre-Christian carved symbols  
- Makeshift nest: blanket, lamp, more stolen books  
- Journal (Douglas Kimball's handwriting): Entries become increasingly erratic  
  - Early: "Found the site Merriwether mentioned. I can feel it \- the boundary is thin here."  
  - Middle: "Something spoke to me last night. Offered knowledge. Showed me how to see beyond."  
  - Recent: "The transformation hurts but I understand now. I serve a greater purpose. The books are payment. Soon I'll cross completely."

**BENEFIT: Understanding Douglas** \- He's been transformed and is working for the entity. Serves it at night, unaware during the day.

**Occult on symbols**:

- These are genuinely old \- pre-colonial, possibly Native American or older  
- Site was sacred/significant before church built  
- **BENEFIT: Understanding Location** \- This was always a threshold point

**Optional Night Visit** (foolish but thorough):

- If investigators come at night (after 10pm), chance to witness Douglas arriving with books  
- Can follow him, observe transformation, see portal activation  
- **BENEFIT: Full Intelligence** \- Complete understanding of situation  
- **RISK**: Potential encounter if detected, SAN loss witnessing transformation

### PATH C: THE LAKE & WATER FEATURES {#path-c:-the-lake-&-water-features}

**Access**: Open, various investigation points  
**Time Investment**: Varies by activity

#### TIER 1: Lake & Dock {#tier-1:-lake-&-dock}

**Observation** (no roll):

- Lake is unnaturally still and quiet  
- No insects, no fish visible, no frogs  
- Dock has three skiffs in good repair

**Natural World or Science**:

- Ecosystem is wrong \- lake should have life, especially in summer  
- Water is contaminated or... something else is wrong  
- **BENEFIT: Understanding** \- Water here is affected by dimensional proximity

**Spot Hidden at dock**:

- One skiff has damp floorboards despite not being used today  
- Wet boot prints (large, fade quickly) leading toward house

**If investigators take skiff out**:

- **Spot Hidden in boat**: Submerged stone structure visible \~50 yards out  
- **Dive/Swim**: Old foundation under water, covered in slick algae  
- **Touching algae**: SAN check 0/1 \- inexplicable revulsion, feeling of wrongness  
- **Occult**: Submerged structure might be original site, older than church  
- **BENEFIT: Understanding** \- Original threshold was at lake, church built to seal/control it

#### TIER 2: Fountain & Garden (Combined) {#tier-2:-fountain-&-garden-(combined)}

**Spot Hidden**:

- Two dead birds near fountain (no wounds, died \~24 hours ago)  
- Another dead bird in hedges

**Natural World**:

- Birds died simultaneously, possibly from shock or heart failure  
- No environmental cause apparent

**Open-Minded Character**:

- Standing near fountain creates odd pressure in ears  
- Air feels "thin" somehow

**Skeptical Character**:

- Fountain cherub statuary is medieval in style \- unusually somber for decoration

**Following up** (requires checking multiple times or Hard Spot Hidden):

- Birds die roughly same time every day (10pm)  
- Pattern started three days ago  
- **BENEFIT: Timing** \- Something happens each night at 10pm (portal activation)

#### TIER 3: Water Connection {#tier-3:-water-connection}

**If investigators connect lake, fountain, and pool** (requires investigating all three):

- All water features show similar "wrongness"  
- Property has unusual amount of water features for the region  
- **History or Library Use**: Merriwether property was chosen by Jeremiah Merriwether (1680s) specifically for "qualities of the land"

**BENEFIT: Tactical Knowledge** \- Entity has affinity for water. Avoid fighting near lake if possible. Church is drier, more favorable ground.

## Scene 3: The Saturday Evening Seance {#scene-3:-the-saturday-evening-seance}

**Guaranteed Information Delivery Point**

All guests gather in the "occult hall" after dinner. Ophelia Ashcroft conducts a seance. This scene provides key information to ALL investigators regardless of what they've found so far.

### Seance Structure {#seance-structure}

**Setup**:

- Occult hall, curtains drawn, candles lit  
- Guests seated around large table  
- Ophelia at head, Rupert beside her  
- She calls forth "spirits of this place" to make their presence known

### Phase 1: Theatrical Effects {#phase-1:-theatrical-effects}

**Phase 2: The Watcher Manifests**

**The Watcher:**

It doesn't fully emerge \- it exists in the threshold, half in our world, half in the space beyond. What the investigators perceive:

- A shape that the eye refuses to hold \- constantly shifting  
- Eyes (too many, arranged wrong) that see through every darkness  
- Limbs or tentacles or something else extending from the portal  
- Cold radiating from it  
- A pressure in the mind \- it sees them, knows them, is interested

**SAN Check: 1D3/1D10** for perceiving the Watcher

**The Watcher Speaks:**

It doesn't use words \- information appears in the investigators' minds:

*THE BRIDGE ALMOST COMPLETE. THE WATCHER WILL CROSS. KNOWLEDGE AWAITS THOSE WHO SERVE. YOU MAY SERVE OR BE CONSUMED.*

**Entity's Goals:**

- It wants to fully cross into our reality  
- Douglas is its bridge \- a human conduit attuned to both sides  
- The ward-stone, moved from the sealing site to the doorway site, allows the crossing  
- It has been trying to cross for centuries \- the Merriwethers' ancestor stopped it before

**Entity's Weaknesses:**

- Cannot manifest fully in bright light (lanterns force it back)  
- Lake water causes it pain (it was sealed BY the lake)  
- Without Douglas as bridge, it cannot cross completely  
- Without the ward-stone, the portal collapses (but entity might be trapped on wrong side)

**Phase 3: Resolution Options**

Investigators can resolve the confrontation through several paths:

**OPTION A: DESTROY THE WARD-STONE**

The ward-stone can be destroyed by:

- Smashing it (crowbar, heavy object, requires 2 rounds of effort)  
- Pushing the altar over onto it (3 people, 1 round, or 1 person with crowbar as lever)  
- Hurling it into bright light while the entity is touching it (Hard Throw, causes feedback)

**Results:**

- Portal collapses violently  
- The entity is severed from our world \- trapped on the other side  
- **Douglas:** Without bridge assistance, he may survive but is permanently changed (if investigators pull him away from the portal before destruction) OR he's pulled through with the entity (if they don't intervene)  
- **SAN Reward: \+1D6**

**OPTION B: RETURN THE WARD-STONE TO THE LAKE**

If investigators learned about the paired sites, they can reseal the threshold by returning the ward-stone to its original position.

**Requirements:**

- Remove ward-stone from altar (Douglas/Entity will resist)  
- Transport it to lake (20-minute journey while potentially pursued)  
- Submerge it in the original alcove location

**How to accomplish this:**

- Subdue or incapacitate Douglas (combat or binding ritual)  
- Negotiate with entity (see Coercion below)  
- Distract entity with light/lake water while others grab stone

**Results:**

- Threshold reseals completely  
- Entity cannot access this location again  
- **Douglas:** Returns to fully human if stone is resealed while he's still on our side. If investigators complete the resealing while he's in the church, he collapses unconscious but recovers over days.  
- **SAN Reward: \+1D8**

**OPTION C: COERCE THE ENTITY**

If investigators have leverage (threaten destruction of ward-stone, have Douglas at gunpoint, have ritual to bind the entity), they can negotiate.

**What the Entity Offers:**

- Knowledge (gain Cthulhu Mythos skill, but at SAN cost)  
- "I will release the bridge. Take the stone. I will wait for another opportunity."  
- Warning: "Others will seek this place. I am not the only thing watching."

**What Investigators Can Demand:**

- Release Douglas completely  
- Retreat and allow resealing  
- Information about other threats

**Psychology (Hard) on Entity:** It's immortal and patient. It doesn't NEED to cross tonight. If truly threatened, it will retreat and wait for another opportunity rather than risk destruction or permanent sealing.

**Results (if Entity retreats):**

- Portal remains but inactive  
- Ward-stone can be disposed of safely  
- Douglas is released, severely traumatized  
- **Warning:** This is not a permanent solution \- the entity will try again, perhaps through other means  
- **SAN Reward: \+1D4**

**OPTION D: THE BINDING RITUAL**

If investigators learned the binding ritual and have 3 participants:

**Requirements:**

- 3 people chanting (15 minutes, or 5 minutes if Ophelia leads)  
- Circle drawn in salt around altar  
- Must maintain concentration (any participant taking damage breaks the ritual)

**What happens:**

- Entity is trapped within the circle  
- Cannot affect anything outside the boundary  
- Investigators can then destroy ward-stone, reseal threshold, or leave entity bound while they decide

**This creates OPTIONS** \- the binding doesn't solve the problem, but it neutralizes the immediate threat and lets investigators choose their resolution.

**Results:**

- Entity bound until circle broken  
- Investigators choose next steps with time to think  
- **SAN Reward: \+1D3** for successfully binding, additional reward based on final resolution

**OPTION E: COMBAT**

If investigators choose to fight:

**Douglas:**

- Partially transformed, fights with unnatural strength and speed  
- STR 16, DEX 14, HP 14  
- Attacks: Claw (1D6+1D4), or grapple  
- **Weakness:** Responds to his name, hesitates if addressed as himself (grants \+1 die to attacks against him for one round if Psychology success calling to his humanity)  
- **If reduced to 0 HP:** Collapses, entity connection severs, portal destabilizes

**The Watcher (partial manifestation):**

- Cannot be truly killed while portal exists  
- Tentacle attacks: 1D8 damage, reach  
- Cold aura: Anyone within 10 feet takes 1 HP cold damage per round  
- **Weakness:** Bright light forces it to retreat partially (lanterns held toward it prevent advancement, flares blind it for 1D3 rounds)  
- **Weakness:** Lake water causes 1D6 damage per contact, makes it recoil  
- **If portal destroyed:** Entity is banished

**Combat is SURVIVABLE but COSTLY** \- expect injuries, potential death if investigators have no protections or preparations. With full preparations (protective circles, light sources, lake water, multiple weapons), combat is challenging but manageable.

If investigators are losing badly, or if they've been respectful to the Merriwether family's tragedy:

**Sarah's spirit manifests.**

A younger woman's shape appears near her grave (visible through church gaps). She's not fully present but her voice carries:

This provides a dramatic out if the party is overwhelmed, but it should feel earned through their interactions with the family.

# Protective Symbols: Tactical Reference {#protective-symbols:-tactical-reference}

## Acquiring the Knowledge {#acquiring-the-knowledge}

### From "Geometries of Protection": {#from-"geometries-of-protection":}

The book describes three distinct protective applications. An investigator can learn them through:

**Quick Study (30 minutes):** Requires an Occult roll

- Success: Learns one application of choice  
- Hard Success: Learns two applications  
- Extreme Success: Learns all three  
- Failure: Misunderstands the geometry; any symbols drawn are flawed and non-functional (Keeper should not reveal this until the symbol fails)

**Thorough Study (2 hours):** Requires an Occult roll with bonus die

- Learns all first application even on failure  
- Hard success: Learns second  
- Extreme success: Learns third  
- Also learns the "Principle of Opposition" (see below)

**Cost:** Reading and understanding the geometries costs 1 SAN (the patterns are subtly wrong to human perception, understanding why they work requires accepting uncomfortable truths about reality).

---

## The Three Applications {#the-three-applications}

### 1\. The Elder Sign (Stationary Ward) {#1.-the-elder-sign-(stationary-ward)}

**What it is:** A specific five-pointed geometric figure with internal angles that "disagree" with normal space.

**Drawing Requirements:**

- Time: 5 minutes per sign  
- Materials: Any marking substance (chalk, paint, blood, carved into surface)  
- Skill: Occult roll to draw correctly  
- With salt as the medium: Roll becomes one difficulty level easier

**Effect:** The Elder Sign creates a point that entities from beyond cannot willingly touch or cross. Functions like a magical "wall anchor point."

**In Practice:**

- Draw on a doorway threshold: Entity cannot pass through that door  
- Draw on the floor: Entity cannot step on that spot  
- Draw on yourself or another person: Entity cannot touch that person directly (but can still throw objects, collapse structures, attack through minions)

**Limitations:**

- Each sign only protects a roughly 5-foot diameter  
- Entity CAN destroy the sign indirectly (knock over the object it's drawn on, splash water on chalk, send Douglas to scuff it)  
- Signs drawn on skin fade after 1 hour (sweat, movement disrupts the geometry)  
- Does not affect Douglas (he's human enough that the sign doesn't recognize him as "other")

**Failure Mode:** If the Occult roll failed during drawing (unknown to player), the sign looks correct but is geometrically flawed. Entity hesitates for one round when approaching (appears to work), then passes through normally.

---

### 2\. The Containment Circle (Binding Perimeter) {#2.-the-containment-circle-(binding-perimeter)}

**What it is:** A closed geometric figure that creates a barrier entities cannot cross in either direction.

**Drawing Requirements:**

- Time: 15 minutes minimum (faster drawing increases difficulty)  
- Materials: Continuous line required (salt is traditional and reduces difficulty; chalk works; blood works but costs 1 HP per 10 feet of circle)  
- Size: Minimum 6-foot diameter, maximum 30-foot diameter  
- Skill: Occult roll to close the circle correctly  
- With salt: Roll is normal difficulty  
- Without salt: Roll is Hard difficulty  
- Rushed (5 minutes): Roll becomes one difficulty level harder

**Effect:** Nothing supernatural can cross the circle's boundary while it remains unbroken. This works both ways:

- Draw around the entity: It's trapped inside  
- Draw around yourselves: Entity cannot reach you, but you can't leave either  
- Draw around the altar: Entity cannot access the ward-stone

**In Practice \- Church Scenarios:**

*Defensive Circle:* Party draws circle around themselves in church corner. Entity cannot reach them. From this position they can:

- Throw lake water at the entity (ranged attacks cross the barrier)  
- Fire the Luger at Douglas  
- Attempt to negotiate  
- Wait for dawn (entity weakens at threshold moments)  
- Plan their next move

*Offensive Circle:* Party draws circle around the altar (requires approaching and working near the entity \- dangerous). If completed:

- Entity cannot access the ward-stone  
- Portal cannot be completed  
- Entity must negotiate, retreat, or find way to break the circle

*Trapping Circle:* If entity is lured or forced into a specific area, party can attempt to draw circle around it. This is extremely dangerous (requires being close to entity while drawing) but fully contains it.

**Limitations:**

- ANY break in the line destroys the effect entirely  
- Douglas can break the circle (he's human enough to cross, then scuff the line)  
- Entity can attempt to trick or coerce someone into breaking the line  
- Physical damage to the floor/ground can break the line  
- Circle provides no protection against mundane threats (Douglas can still attack across it)

**Failure Mode:** If the Occult roll failed, the circle has an invisible geometric flaw. Effect lasts for 1D6 rounds, then fails suddenly at the worst possible moment.

---

### 3\. The Banishment Glyph (Aggressive Ward) {#3.-the-banishment-glyph-(aggressive-ward)}

**What it is:** A complex symbol that actively repels rather than passively blocks.

**Drawing Requirements:**

- Time: 10 minutes  
- Materials: Must include salt AND another substance (chalk, ash, blood)  
- Size: Approximately 2 feet diameter  
- Skill: Hard Occult roll (this is the most complex geometry)  
- If the drawer has thoroughly studied the book (2 hours): Normal Occult roll instead

**Effect:** When an entity comes within 10 feet of the completed glyph, it experiences something like pain. It must make a POW roll or be forced to retreat to at least 20 feet away. Even on success, it suffers visible distress and attacks made from within 10 feet of the glyph are at one difficulty level harder.

**In Practice:**

- Draw near the church entrance: Entity has difficulty leaving the altar area  
- Draw between party and entity: Creates a "safe zone" of 10 feet around the glyph  
- Draw multiple glyphs: Each one forces a separate POW roll, potentially herding the entity

**Limitations:**

- Only forces retreat, doesn't contain or damage  
- Entity can still act at range (thrown objects, psychic pressure)  
- POW roll means strong entities may resist  
- Takes longer to draw than other symbols  
- Most likely to be drawn incorrectly (Hard roll)

**Failure Mode:** Flawed glyph attracts entity attention rather than repelling it. Entity is drawn to investigate the glyph, may realize the party is attempting magical defense.

---

## The Principle of Opposition (Bonus from Thorough Study) {#the-principle-of-opposition-(bonus-from-thorough-study)}

Investigators who spend the full 2 hours studying learn an additional principle:

**"That which binds may also be bound. The geometries function bidirectionally. A sign that repels the outsider will equally repel that which the outsider has touched."**

**Meaning:**

- Elder Signs affect partially-transformed Douglas (he hesitates, feels discomfort, may be blocked if transformation is advanced enough)  
- Containment circles can trap Douglas after midnight (when his transformation is more complete)  
- This gives the party a non-lethal option for dealing with Douglas: contain him until dawn, when he becomes more human

**How to Use:** If Douglas is contained in a circle overnight, by dawn he's mostly human again. Weak, confused, possibly reachable. The entity's grip on him weakens when it can't reinforce the connection.

## Failure Consequences Table {#failure-consequences-table}

| Roll Result | Immediate Appearance | Actual Effect |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Success | Symbol glows faintly when entity approaches | Functions as intended |
| Failure | Symbol looks correct | Works for 1 round, then fails |
| Fumble | Symbol looks correct | Attracts entity attention; may enrage it |

**Keeper Note:** Don't tell players their roll failed unless it's a fumble. Let them believe the symbol works until the entity tests it. This creates genuine tension—did I draw it right?

## Combining Symbols with Other Benefits {#combining-symbols-with-other-benefits}

**Symbols \+ Light:** If party has lanterns driving entity back AND protective symbols, they can essentially control the space. Entity is funneled into areas without symbols and away from light. This creates a "kill zone" where the party has maximum advantage.

**Symbols \+ Lake Water:** Symbols force entity to stay in certain areas; lake water damages it while it's there. Combination allows party to wear down the entity's manifestation.

**Symbols \+ Binding Ritual:** The binding ritual from "Methods of Banishment" requires a containment circle already drawn. If party has both the symbol knowledge AND the ritual knowledge, they can contain the entity permanently (until circle is broken). This is the "capture" option.

**Symbols \+ Altar Destruction:** Draw containment circle around altar, preventing entity from interfering, then destroy altar/ward-stone at leisure. This is the "safe" approach but requires successfully completing the circle near the entity.

## Example Tactical Sequence {#example-tactical-sequence}

*Party has: salt, two lanterns, protective symbol knowledge, one character with Occult 45%*

1. **Approach church.** Draw Elder Sign on door frame before entering (5 min, Occult roll). If entity flees, it cannot exit through this door.  
     
2. **Enter and assess.** Entity and Douglas at altar, portal forming.  
     
3. **Establish position.** Two characters with lanterns advance slowly, driving entity back with light. Third character begins drawing containment circle around the party's corner (15 min with salt).  
     
4. **Entity responds.** Sends Douglas to attack. Douglas is human enough that symbols don't stop him, but Luger can wound him, or physical combat can subdue him.  
     
5. **Circle complete.** Party is protected. From here they can negotiate, wait for dawn, throw lake water at the entity, or plan approach to altar.  
     
6. **Endgame.** One character leaves circle to sprint for altar while others throw lake water and blind entity with lanterns. Character destroys ward-stone. Portal collapses.

This is a HIGH-BENEFIT scenario. Party with minimal preparation faces the same situation with far fewer options and much higher danger.
