# The Paper Chase — Proposed Final Structure (v3)

A **structural blueprint**, not prose. It fixes the spine, timeline, geography,
and benefit economy so the detailed text from
[`../source/paper-chase-refactor.md`](../source/paper-chase-refactor.md) can be
hung back onto it slot-by-slot (see §10, the detail-hanging map). Builds on the
analysis and your edits in [`skeleton-2.md`](skeleton-2.md).

> **Status:** awaiting your sign-off. §9 lists the open decisions that change how
> detail gets hung. Defaults are chosen and marked **[D]**; override any of them.

---

## 1. Design pillars (the rules every beat must obey)

1. **Open-world until one hinge.** All investigation is order-independent and
   non-gating up to the Saturday seance. The seance is the *only* scheduled beat.
2. **Seance = PLOT, investigation = POWER.** The seance guarantees every table
   learns *what/where* (no hard-stall). Investigation is the only source of
   *survival* (wards, allies, weapons, timing, win-conditions). No investigation
   beat may pay out *only* plot the seance gives for free.
3. **Every beat yields a clue or an advantage.** A scene either makes the party
   materially stronger or points at something that will. Pure-mood beats are cut
   or upgraded into a scare-with-payoff.
4. **Soft clock, then hard.** No time pressure before the seance — let the
   sandbox breathe. The seance starts a hard countdown that forces the night trek.
5. **Dread, used sparingly.** 1–2 "is it natural, occult, or *Cthulhu*?" beats
   during the sandbox build fear and create pressure — each also teaches or
   yields something. (Your erratum #2.)
6. **Two asymmetric sandbox windows, not two identical ones.** Friday afternoon =
   orientation + social; Saturday = deep investigation. Avoids the original's sin
   of re-offering the same paths twice.

## 2. Canonical backstory (reconciled)

The single source of truth — supersedes the contradictory versions in the source.

- A pre-colonial **threshold** lies beneath the **lake**. A Merriwether ancestor
  sealed it with a carved **ward-stone** in a submerged alcove. The ruined
  **church (St Crispin's)** stands on the *paired* site — the "door," held shut
  only while the stone stays in the lake.
- **Sarah Merriwether** drowned in the lake in **1919** investigating the
  anomaly. Her father **Rupert**, consumed by guilt, has researched how to reach
  her, and shared that research with houseguest **Douglas Kimball** (ex–science
  teacher, occult researcher).
- **Douglas**, touched through the research, performed his own ritual at the
  lake, was **captured** by the entity behind the stone, and — over the week
  before play — **retrieved the ward-stone** and installed it at the church in a
  **reversed** configuration. He is becoming the **bridge** for the **Watcher**.
  His nightly church trips, stolen books, and nest predate the party's arrival.
- **The anchor model (the key contradiction, now resolved):** the **ward-stone is
  the key** — *return it to the lake* = reseal (best outcome), *destroy it* =
  collapse the portal (entity likely trapped on the far side). The **grimoire**
  buried under the altar is the **instructions** (it tells you *how* to reseal),
  **not** itself the thing to destroy. All "destroy the buried book" lines (Rupert's
  office, the library, Sarah at the seance) are rewritten to "the book tells you how."

**The hidden clock of NPC actions across the weekend** (what's happening off-screen):

| When | Off-screen event | On-screen evidence it leaves |
|---|---|---|
| Week before | Douglas retrieves stone, preps church, transforms nightly | Empty lake alcove; dead-water lake; church nest (if scouted); missing library books |
| **Fri night** | **Bertrand**, guilt-stricken, drives to the lake to try his own ritual to reach Sarah; finds the water *wrong*, panics, dents the Rolls | Fresh mud (grey lake clay) on boots/undercarriage; fender damage; **mud indoors Saturday that wasn't there at the 8 pm Friday cleaning** |
| Sat, escalating | Douglas's transformation nears completion; the Watcher pushes to cross | Worsening dread beats (fountain, birds, memorial) |
| **Sat ~8 pm** | The seance; the Watcher reaches through Ophelia; Douglas is exposed | **He flees to the church.** Hard clock begins. |
| Sat night | The crossing culminates | The finale |

> This makes **Friday night active** (your item 4) and gives players the
> diegetic "**anything after the 8 pm cleaning is suspicious**" heuristic (your
> item 5) without anyone explaining it. **[D]** The stone is *already gone* when
> the party arrives (simplest to run); Bertrand's Friday trip is his own parallel
> grief, not the mechanical trigger. See §9-A.

## 3. Master run-of-show (the new timeline)

| Time | Beat | Type | Purpose / yields |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fri 10 am** | Arrival, settle into rooms | Framing | Meet staff; the grounds open up |
| **Fri afternoon** | **Sandbox #1 — orientation** | Open (light) | Grounds & first impressions; meet guests; seed one hook per PC. *Lighter than Saturday.* |
| **Fri ~7 pm** | **Formal dinner** | Scripted social | Big lore drop + NPC interchange; **chances to ally or alienate** (esp. Bertrand, Ophelia); Rupert frames the gathering & announces tomorrow's seance |
| **Fri night** | Estate cleaned at 8 pm; **Bertrand's lake trip** (near-miss possible) | Soft / optional dread | Late-wandering PCs may glimpse a figure at the dock + movement in the water (tension, not combat). Sets the mud clue. |
| **Sat 8 am** | **Breakfast** | Scripted social | NPCs haggard (Bertrand, Rupert); **fresh mud** where it shouldn't be; direction-setting without spoon-feeding |
| **Sat day** | **Sandbox #2 — deep investigation** | Open (main) | All paths live; **1–2 dread beats** fire here; this is where most advantages are earned |
| **Sat 6 pm** | Dinner | Framing | Last social beat; tension high |
| **Sat ~8 pm** | **THE SEANCE — the hinge** | Scripted | Plot floor delivered to all; **Douglas exposed → flees**; hard clock starts |
| **Sat night** | **Douglas's room** (the one sensible pre-church stop) → **prep/loadout** | Short open beat | Cash in benefits; final timing intel (your F3 note) |
| **Sat night** | **The long trek to St Crispin's** | Scripted dread | A real journey through dark woods — distance is the cost (your erratum #1) |
| **Sat night** | **The church finale** (3 phases + resolutions) | Scripted set-piece | Confront Douglas + the Watcher; resolve |
| After | **Denouement** | Framing | Douglas's fate, the family, rewards |

**The forced-march dilemma (the hard clock, encoded):** the seance makes clear
the crossing **culminates tonight**. Going *now* (≈10 pm–2 am): the Watcher is at
its strongest **but** Douglas is still partly human and **savable**, and the
crossing is still stoppable. *Waiting for dawn*: the Watcher weakens at the dawn
threshold **but** Douglas likely transitions fully (lost), and the crossing may
complete before sunrise. So the party is pushed to march at night *despite*
knowing night is more dangerous — exactly your item 7.

## 6. Dread beats (the 1–2 scares)

Keep **two active at most**; #1 is the keystone (scare *and* payoff).

1. **The Fountain reach** *(keystone — Saturday day, accessible).* Something
   beneath the water lashes out (a tentacle / coil) — brief, genuinely dangerous,
   SAN check. Survivors learn the Watcher can **reach through any still water on
   the grounds**, and that **heavy leather (an apron, a coat) blunts its grip** —
   a usable finale defense. The ambiguity ("a pike? a prank? a *thing*?") is the
   point. *Replaces the cut fountain flavour from skeleton-2.*
2. **Sarah's Memorial** *(secondary — near stables, accessible).* The sculpted
   relief of Sarah's face weeps lake-water / its eyes track the viewer / a
   whispered fragment in her voice ("he didn't listen…"). A jump-scare and a SAN
   tickle that **foreshadows Sarah-as-ally** at the finale. Optional minor threat.
3. **Bertrand at the lake** *(optional — Friday night near-miss).* Late wanderers
   glimpse a figure at the dock and movement in the water. Tension, not combat;
   converts to fresh evidence regardless.

Rule: never more than one dread beat per sandbox window unless players go looking.

## 7. The seance (the hinge) — structural requirements

Carried from the source, with two guardrails:
- **Plot only.** It delivers the floor — *church · the stone (return/break it) ·
  the Watcher · Douglas is the bridge · Sarah opposes it* — and **Ophelia as
  ally**. It does **not** grant survival advantages (those stay in the sandbox).
- **Point of no return.** Douglas flees and **does not return**. (Kills the
  Sunday-breakfast reappearance.) The hard clock starts the instant he bolts.

---

## 8. The church finale — structural requirements

Three phases + resolution menu, essentially as the source has them (it's the
best-built part). Structural notes only:
- **Phase 1 — Negotiation/leverage:** humanity-appeal to Douglas is *easier or
  guaranteed* if the party built rapport at dinner/library (A7). Entity fears for
  the stone → coercion leverage.
- **Phase 2 — Watcher manifests:** the weaknesses named here must be **exactly**
  the ones earnable in the sandbox (light, lake water, *now also* the
  leather-apron grip defense) so prep visibly pays off.
- **Phase 3 — Resolutions:** destroy stone / **return stone to lake (best, saves
  Douglas)** / coerce / bind / fight, each gated to specific banked benefits.
  **Sarah's intervention** is the safety net — *earned* via family/memorial
  roleplay, never automatic.
