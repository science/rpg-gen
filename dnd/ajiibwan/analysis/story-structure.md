# Ajiibwan — Story Structure & World Read

A structural analysis of the *Deserts of Ajiibwan* campaign, built from the core
homebrew docs (see [`inventory.md`](inventory.md)). Purpose: give a GM (and the
adventure-manager tooling) a fast, accurate map of **what the world is, what the prior
party did, and what's left open** for the new party. It's an open-world sandbox, so
"sequence" is soft — captured here as the prior party's *actual* path, not a fixed rail.

> Source docs: `Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands`, `Lore - Primary Source materials`,
> `New Players Guide`, `How to Create a Character`, `Travel/Gaming/Trading Rules`,
> `GM Game Notes - Ajiibwan Meetup game`, `Journal - Ajiibwan Meetup game - Party Notes`.
> Treat proper nouns as authoritative-but-verify; some come from fast reads of 100k+ words.

## 1. The world in one paragraph

The **Ajiibwan Desert** is a harsh frontier at the edge of the **Vanic Imperium**.
Tone is **Conan / Game of Thrones**, not whimsical fantasy: lethal combat, scarce and
*dangerous* magic, historically-grounded cultures (Arabic/Hindi/Sinhala-flavored naming),
moral compromise as a constant. Two dead ancient civilizations underlie everything — the
high-magic **Bokkaran** and the giant-led **Ukar Federation** — whose millennia-long war
blighted the land into desert and left a **curse of "magical consequences"** that makes
ordinary arcane magic backfire. Their ruins, constructs, and buried powers are the engine
of nearly every adventure.

## 2. Geography & major regions

Gateway and home base is **Tel A'bib** (Imperial frontier town: market, New Moon Tavern,
temple, weak/corrupt garrison). Outward from there:

- **The deep desert** — Blistering Dunes (deadly central waste), the **Great Oasis** /
  **Air Spire**, oasis settlements **Sia** and **Farrav'n** (gnoll), scattered ruins.
- **The Saragubi Desert** (east/south) — homeland of the proud **Tugari** warrior tribes;
  the *Pyramid of the Lost King* / Kalinore arc.
- **The Granite Empire** (west) — isolationist dwarven power; capital port **Hothme**
  (under the desert), ruled by the **m'Thorsz Dhallak Carn**; forbids Vanic citizens.
  Contains **Sefurah Springs / Al Faiz** (the Ukar source-tree) and the Slumbering Titan.
- **The Holy Cities (Bluffside)** — six interconnected cities (Zakutar, Zakuta, Ell Bastis,
  Sheloah, Nabi Nageab, Birjari + Inferna Wadi) over the secret underground **Sem La Vah**.
- **The Sea of Souls** — an underground network of caverns whose **invisible water puts
  the unprotected instantly to sleep**; the desert's hidden "trade highway," gated by
  permits and the guardian lizard **Yormon/Kazool**. Connects Hothme, B'rath, the Holy
  Cities, and the lost Bokkaran city **Bokkaral**.

Sites are placed on a **hex map** (folder names carry coordinates like `1810`, `1119`) and
carry an **adventure index (#1–29)** and **level range** — the world's built-in catalogue.

## 3. Cosmology & history (the deep lore)

- **The Bokkaran** — ancient high-magic people, cursed to take damage from daylight,
  split into **Elgar** (light wizards, nature-harmony) and **Polgar** (necromancers).
  The Polgar's war-magic **poisoned the ley lines**, producing the magical-consequences
  curse, and they allied with then betrayed the Ukar. Effectively extinct; left behind
  **language engines, white-steel constructs, the Sea of Souls, and ruins**.
- **The Ukar Federation** — older still, included **60-ft Titans**; elemental/earth magic,
  sign language, underground stone palaces. Warred with the Bokkaran for millennia until
  banished to an ethereal plane ~2,000 years ago. Left **portals** (e.g. Afra's temple),
  artifacts, and the **Al Faiz** source-tree.
- **The Great War → the desert** — mutual annihilation blighted the region into wasteland.
- **The curse of magical consequences** — the defining magic constraint (see §6).
- **Deities/fey**: **Tanil** (archfey of the wild, warlock patron), **Imroth** (god of
  performance), **Anauld** (local holy days), the **Doornian** religion of Tel A'bib.
- **Mythic open threats**: a dismembered **Slumbering Titan** imprisoned beneath B'rath by
  a Bokkaran needle-device; the lost city **Bokkaral** (may hold living Bokkaran).

## 4. Factions & peoples

- **Vanic Imperium** — the PCs' civilization; controls Tel A'bib; weak past the frontier.
- **Granite Empire** — isolationist dwarven west; ruled from Hothme; rich merchant houses
  (Cascabel, Gideon, Vedek, Zingolfin) and the Scholar's Guild at **Valeska's Needle**.
- **Tugari tribes** — Saragubi warrior nomads, bound to the Empire by the **Millennial
  Pact**; deadly to fight below ~L10, but winnable through respect.
- **The Black Water Cult** — the recurring antagonist: a Church-Inquisition-meets-Scientology
  organization that reveres the invisible Sea-of-Souls water; corrupts officials, disrupts
  trade, terrorizes towns into dependence. 500+ strong in the Saragubi.
- **Lilth nomads** — Bokkaran-descended gypsies replanting **Storm Orchids** to heal the
  desert; ambiguous allies.
- Smaller players: Tel A'bib merchants (Elmingwed, the slaver Gala Dak), gnolls of Farrav'n,
  the Drow agents tied to organ/slave trafficking.

## 5. The prior campaign — actual play arc

Ran ~90 sessions across in-world Year 641 Day 10 → Year 642. Rough sequence the prior
party followed (open-world, so order is indicative):

1. **Arrival & first forays** (Tel A'bib) — Oasis of Sia, B'rath fire-beetle caverns,
   first Black Water Cult contact near Durgan's Rest.
2. **Tomb-and-ruin phase** — Sia sphinx tomb, Eye of Kings, Forsaken Pyramid (Bokkaran
   language engine), Kantaron Summit / Tinkerer's Workshop, Radiant Necropolis.
3. **Bridged City phase** — the Academy/Shunned arc; first contact with **Yormon** and the
   villain **Maghiel** (hunting the **Clasp of Vergen**).
4. **Holy Cities & the slave/organ network** — Dimark spell-engine, Tanil's Glade (PCs gain
   Bokkaran Warlock level), busting **Gala Dak's** trafficking compound (gained a Sea of
   Souls boat + base).
5. **Sea of Souls opens up** — voyages to B'rath, Crystal Towers / Three Kings hall.
6. **Granite Empire trade & demon incursion** — Hothme trade runs; **Duroigh** the
   necromancer summons an Abyssal demon at Kantaron; the demon **escapes**.
7. **Kantaron deep / Saragubi** — defeat of the **Nalfeshnee** & undead Duroigh; the
   Saragubi Black Water compound; Kalinore's tomb arc.
8. **Hothme / Granite Empire deep** — Clan Gideon alliance, Afra's Tomb, undead King Donol.
9. **Afra's awakening** — unlock the Ukar tomb; **Maeve (PC) killed by a giant**; **Afra**
   (Ukar entity) awakened, opening the **Ukar portal network**.

## 6. Major throughlines (still live)

1. **The Black Water Cult** — degraded but not broken; leadership above "Chazim" unknown.
2. **The Bokkaran/Ukar mystery** — *why* the ley-lines were poisoned, and whether the
   desert can be **healed** (the Lilth's Storm Orchids; Ukar/Mesos both want it cleansed).
3. **Maghiel & the Clasp of Vergen** — villain still at large, goals beyond the Clasp murky.
4. **The escaped Abyssal demon** — loose in the region.
5. **The Ukar portal network** — newly opened via Afra; scope and gatekeepers unknown.
6. **The Slumbering Titan / Bokkaral** — dormant mega-threats / discoveries.

## 7. How the open world is run (GM operating model)

This is the model the adventure-manager should support:

- **Hex-mapped catalogue.** Each site = adventure index # + hex coord + level range.
  New adventures are spun up where the party chooses to go, scaled to party level.
- **Rotating roster.** No fixed party; players bring a character each session; NPCs
  (Jiyloo, Dain) fill gaps. Implies the tool tracks a *pool* of PCs, not one party line-up.
- **Adventure seeding from the world.** Hooks come from NPCs (Essen's rescue jobs,
  Elmingwed's merchant intel), rumor/lore from priests & scholars, and **artifact-driven
  delves** (recovering a Bokkaran artifact wakes constructs → dungeon). Factional friction
  (Black Water vs. Lilth vs. Imperial vs. Ukar) generates conflict zones.
- **Time & travel matter.** In-world calendar (360-day year) is tracked per day; overland
  travel and Sea-of-Souls voyages have real costs (potions vs. sleep water, camel upkeep,
  trade economics).
- **Downtime/trade economy.** Bases, rents, caravan trading, gambling minigames — a whole
  between-adventures layer.

## 8. House rules that shape design

- **No standard Wizard/Sorcerer/Warlock**, and no spell those classes could cast (even
  cantrips) — the magical-consequences curse. Arcane power must be *earned in-world* as
  **Bokkaran Wizard/Warlock** (engines, patrons like Tanil, studying texts) or **Ukar**
  (bloodline/patron). This is the single most distinctive mechanic.
- **Start at level 2**, level to 3 before play; **standard array** (15/14/13/12/10/8).
- **Magical consequences**: casting cursed magic unprotected triggers wild, often harmful
  effects; mitigated by Bokkaran charms, casting below capacity, or an anti-miscast cantrip.
- **Sea of Souls**: invisible water = instant sleep unless protected (Bokkaran charm /
  ritual / item); drowning rules apply to the protected-but-submerged.
- **Subsystems**: Dragonchess, trading (3-skill resolution), drinking/racing/wrestling
  contests, structured overland-travel prep.

## 9. The Adventure Template (schema to mirror)

`Adventure Template.docx` is the GM's own scenario skeleton — the basis for
`scenarios/*.yaml`:

1. **Title**
2. **Background** — context/history leading to the situation
3. **Overall Goal** — the dramatic objective
4. **Motivations** — why the party cares (hooks/rewards)
5. **Obstacles** — NPCs, monsters, hazards, social conflict
6. **Narrative Structure** — Pre-Game / In-Game / Post-Game
7. **Major Scenes** — key encounters & locations
8. **Rewards** — treasure, XP, reputation, story progress
9. **Enemies** — stat blocks + Roll20 attack macros

## 10. Hooks for the new party (design starting points)

Concrete, lore-grounded jumping-off points the tooling can pay off:

- Pick up a **dangling throughline** from §6 (cult cells regrouping; the loose demon; a
  newly-opened Ukar portal disgorging something; Maghiel's next move).
- Use an **unwritten catalogued site** (folders with maps/PDF but no homebrew text — *Eye
  of Kings*, *Oasis of Sia*, *Port of Dakhalla*, the *City of* sites, *Beneath the Festered
  Sun*) as fresh territory for new PCs, scaled to their level.
- Lean on **inherited NPCs & base** (Elmingwed, Jiyloo, the Tel A'bib compound, Clan Gideon
  trade ties) so new players plug into a living world rather than a blank slate.
- Advance the **desert-healing** question (Storm Orchids / Al Faiz) as a long campaign spine.
