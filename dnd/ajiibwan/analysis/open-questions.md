## A. Cosmology & deep history (true canon)

### ⭐ A1. Who was the Bokkaran's ancient enemy — the Ukar, the Titans, or both?
The world-lore doc frames it as **Bokkaran vs. the Ukar** (a federation/"ideology, not a
race," organized around three thrones: titan, giant, humanoid). The Titan **Mesos's**
in-world testimony frames it as **Bokkaran vs. the Titans**, who were once *unified* with
the Bokkaran before the Bokkaran grew greedy. These don't cleanly agree.
- Is "Ukar" the whole federation (which *includes* Titans), with the Titans just its most
  powerful members? Or are Ukar and Titans distinct?
- Is Mesos a reliable narrator, or is his "we were once one people" version a Titan myth?

> **Answer:**
The Ukar are both a race of Titans and the name of a federation of species who collaborated with the Titans in a shared cultural expanse. Some city-state ruins will have three symbolic stone throne chairs - one big enough for a 60' titan, one big enough for a standard giant, and one big enough for a small/medium creature: this symbolizes that all three have power, even if the origins of the Ukar culture are Titans, and titan members have tremendous individual powers. 

Mesos is a confused narrator. Some of what he says is true, but some is hopeful. He's been imprisoned and disembodied for 2000 years and so has some cognitive decline. Should he be re-corporated, his memory would improve somewhat, but not completely: much is lost.


### ⭐ A2. "The Slumbering Titan" — is that Afra or Mesos? (Are there two titans?)
There appear to be **two** dormant titans: **Afra**, awakened by the party in the Granite
Empire ruins (the *Slumbering Titan Adventure* → opens the Ukar portal network); and
**Mesos**, the "Titan King" imprisoned in a **Dream Catcher** beneath **B'rath** (the
*Freeing Mesos* arc). The label "Slumbering Titan" currently points at both in different
places.
- Confirm: Afra and Mesos are distinct individuals? Which (if either) is "*the* Slumbering
  Titan"? What's the relationship between them (peers, ruler/architect, rivals)?

> **Answer:**
Afra is sleeping in a ruined Ukar complex west of Hothme in the Granite Empire's "half" of the desert. Mesos is relatively easy to find, located not far from Tel A'bib. Afra is tremendously difficult to find and is a late stage character to encounter, dropping a ton of lore if she is approached correctly: and opening the Ukar "tree portal network" which is their equivalent of the Sea of Souls. This is a teleportation network facilitated by a magical tree that was developed by Afra herself. Portals in this network are found throughout the world, but operating them requires magic: Afra's Spark, which must be incorporated physically into a creature's body in order to allow them to use the network.


### A3. Elgar / Polgar vs. the "Nurian Necropolis schism" — same split?
Confirmed real: the Bokkaran split into **Elgar** (light wizards, nature-harmony) and
**Polgar** (necromancers) (*Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands*). The **Nurian Necropolis** is
described as evidence of "a split between nature-enhancing wizards and death-focused
necromancers." Safe to treat the Necropolis as a **Polgar** site / the schism's monument?

> **Answer:**
The Necropolis is, at its end-state, a Polgar site. But it also contains history that shows how it wasn't always a Necropolis: attentive investigation shows history in parts of the site that document how the Polgar took power in the Bokkaran empire, and ultimately drove them deeper into the war. One key, dark secret is that the Polgar are the ones who developed the magic that created the Ajiibwan desert itself (which was formerly fertile plains) and left the anti-magic poison that affects the entire world: corrupting the ley lines and preventing safe use of deep magic. 

### A4. Black Thorns vs. "Bokkara statue disease" / "The Thorn"
The Polgar's curse is described as **Black Thorns** (corrupted Bokkaran artifact fragments,
chaotic necromancy). Separately the docs mention a "Bokkara statue disease" and "The
Thorn." Are these the same phenomenon (petrification curse) under different names, or
distinct things?

> **Answer:**
Not petrification: this is historical evidence that can be unearthed that gives clues to what is going on with Maghiel. She gained great power but also behaves in evil and sinister ways. The source is that she has been impaled by a Black Thorn. In the first play-through, the end of the story reveals her black thorn, after she has been killed. The thorn is returned to the Central, old life-tree, and incorporates there, helping restore the tree to health and functioning (it was almost killed by the Polgar magic that poisoned the ley lines).

## B. Identity & duplication (same entity, or two?)

### ⭐ B1. Maghiel — one villain, several names & a gender flip
Captured as **Maghiel / Magdaliel / Maghiel Erdot / "MG"**, and described once as a
**female Drow** and elsewhere as a male archmage/ranger. Currently merged into one
arch-antagonist with "disputed identity."
- Canonical name spelling? Gender? Race/class (Drow wizard)? Is "Erdot" a surname or a
  separate person?

> **Answer:**
Maghiel is the character name. Drow battle mage wizard. Female. The party doesn't know it for a long time, but she is acting under the powers and spell of a Black thorn that is splintered into her chest, hidden under her armor. I don't have a last name for her yet, but we can develop one. I'm not sure where Erdot came from, but let's drop it.

### ⭐ B2. Durel vs. Duroigh — the Kantaron necromancer(s)
The *Tinkerer's Workshop* docs name **Durel**, the entranced **artificer** summoning the
demon at Kantaron. The GM notes name **Duroigh**, a **necromancer** who is reanimated as
undead and re-fought. Same person (Durel → fell → undead Duroigh), or two different NPCs?

> **Answer:**
Yes - Druel is the artificer, and after he died, Maghiel brought him back and he became Duroigh.


### ⭐ B3. Nalfeshnee or Balor? — the Kantaron demon
The GM notes call the summoned demon a **Nalfeshnee**; one reader labeled it a **Balor**.
These are different creatures in 5e. Which is it (for the stat block when it recurs)?

> **Answer:**
Balor is the demon type, and Nafleshnee is the name of the specific demon who is called.


### B4. Jiyloo — one person or a name collision?
Appears as (a) **Jiyloo Ubbett**, the human barbarian, Feltan's assistant / long-running
party companion; (b) a Saragubi "horseman Jiyloo"; (c) a B'rath "female merchant/guide
Jiyloo." Same recurring NPC, or coincidental same-name NPCs?

> **Answer:**
All the same person - who participates with the party in many different adventures. Sometimes arriving to help from the outside, and sometimes traveling with the party.

### B5. Abet vs. Abet Phera
Two captures: **Abet**, an Imperial Captain of the Guard (Tel A'bib); and **Abet Phera**, a
hired private soldier for the merchant Elmingwed. Same person moonlighting, or two people?

> **Answer:**
Abet name refers to the same person consistently: a captain for Elmingwed's private militia - a key leader for Elmingwed's control of mercantile trading power.

### ▫ B6. Tsendur — orc or half-orc?
The Durgan's Rest Bokkaran scholar is "orc" in one doc, "half-orc" in another. Which?

> **Answer:**
Tsendur is a tame full orc. This makes him seem scary but he's a scholar and magician.

### ⭐ B7. Covent of the Weeping Moon vs. Chandler's Folly (Radiant Necropolis)
Lore says **"(31) Weeping Moon (Chandler's Folly)"** — implying one place. But there are
**two distinct sited folders**: *Covent of the Weeping Moon* (hex 2312, L4) and *Chandler's
Folly — Radiant Necropolis* (hex 2510, L4–6). One site or two? If one, which hex/level is
right?

> **Answer:**
Covent of the Weeping Moon is a marshy, haunted area north east of Durgan's rest, and due north of the Bridged city (much closer to Durgan's rest). It's an area on the east side of the mountains that bracket the Ajiibwan desert. North east of the Covent is the Radiant Necropolis which is an ancient Titan tomb.

### ▫ B8. Eye of Kings vs. the Tomb/Caves of Ancestors
The early (~Day 6) rescue is called "Eye of Kings" in lore and "Tomb/Caves of Ancestors"
in the journal. Same complex?

> **Answer:**
Yes - same complex - different names (lots of places have a local's name and a Vanic official name: Eye of Kings is Vanic, Cave of Ancestors is the local/Doornian name)
