# The Tinkerer's Strings
### Kantaron Summit — the Kantaroz tower / Tinkerer's Workshop
*A level-1 sandbox adventure for the Deserts of Ajiibwan (game-2 reset).*

> **At the table:** 4 PCs at **level 1**, plus **Jiyloo** (NPC, level 1 Barbarian — the
> front-line muscle) and the captive **Jeff** (level 1 Rogue — a combat wildcard who may
> fight on the wrong side or sit tied up). The GM paces fights by adjusting HP on the fly
> (easy→hard→medium across the session), so the stat blocks below are reference, not gospel
> — don't sweat exact DCs.
>
> Built from the Adventure Template (trimmed). Canon notes: **Durel is alive** here (the
> reanimated "Duroigh" was a prior-run end-state); the **Balor, Nalfeshnee, is incomplete**
> — the party fights an immature body + the summoning apparatus, *not* a CR-19 demon;
> **Maghiel is off-screen.**

**Dramatic question:** *Everyone at Kantaron is on someone's strings — Durel on the
demon's, Jeff on his own lies, Orkideh on her past. Whose strings do you cut, and what
cuts back?*

**The real trap of this adventure:** the summoning fight is **hard**, so the temptation is
to avoid the basement — interrogate Jeff, loot the tower, haul him to town, come back
*later*. That's a fair call: the clock is **slow** — weeks to months, not same-day doom
(see *The Kantaron clock* in the Back-to-Tel A'bib branch). But it never stops. Walk away
and the demon keeps growing (thread:kantaron-summoning), announced by ever-bolder construct
raids on the foothills, until Tel A'bib itself is forced to act. **Dealing with it now is a
tough fight; ignoring it is a worse one later.**

## Background

Guided by **Jiyloo** (hired by the merchant-magnate **Elmingwed**), the party tracked
**Orkideh's** old criminal partner — **Jeffrol Giltor**, "Jeff," a half-orc/half-elf — to
**Kantaron Summit**, and caught him slipping out of an ancient **Bokkaran tower
(Kantaroz)** with loot in his pack. *(That capture has already happened — the session opens
with Jeff trussed and the tower still unentered.)*

The tower is the **Tinkerer's Workshop**. Its sole resident, the artificer-magician
**Durel**, learned to command the site's ancient Bokkaran **constructs** and sends them
into the foothills for materials (the construct **Ren** spotted). Durel thinks he's
recovering lost Bokkaran magic. He's wrong: he has fallen under the **mental control of a
demon** — a Balor, *Nalfeshnee* — being **grown a body and summoned in spirit** in the
basement lab. Durel is the demon's hands now.

Beneath all of it, the basement is a hidden **wellhead of the Sea of Souls**: Bokkaran
leyline magic conjures water there and drains it down the mountain toward Tel A'bib, where
it becomes the unseen water of the Sea of Souls. *(Pure flavor/mystery for this adventure —
disrupting the summoning does **not** harm the Sea of Souls at this scale. It's a seed for
later; see `world/lore-and-mysteries.md`.)*

And in Jeff's pack is the thing Orkideh's deal was really about: a **white-metal Bokkaran
bracelet wrought as a long-legged spider.** *(It's a [[item:spider-bracelets|Sea-of-Souls
ward]] — but per the world's old-school rule, nobody can tell that by looking. See Rewards.)*

## Overall Goal

**No single mandated objective** — this is a sandbox node. The live goals:

- **Settle Jeff** — extract his value (the tower, the bracelet, the way down) and decide
  his fate. *The spine.*
- **Crack Kantaroz** — loot and explore the tower; find the way into the lab.
- **Deal with the summoning** — fight through the immature demon and wreck the apparatus.
  Hard, but the longer they wait, the worse it gets.

## The Captive: Jeff (Jeffrol Giltor) — GM dossier
*The most important NPC in the session. A level-1 Rogue. Run him as a survivor, not a
villain.*

**His pitch:** he'd rather throw in with the party than be handed to Elmingwed, whom he
fears. He's useful — he *just looted this tower* and **knows the passphrase to the lab.**
He bargains hard for life and freedom.

**The combat wildcard:** if a fight breaks out and he's loose, he'll act in *his* interest
— stab a distracted PC and bolt, "help" in a way that frees himself, or genuinely fight
beside them if he judges that's his best odds. If he's tied up, the party fights a man down
(no Jeff, and someone may need to mind him). Let that tension ride.

**The bomb he drops (thread:orkideh-secret).** To buy his life he'll tell the party about
him and Orkideh — spun his way:

> *They ran a fiber house — sisal and jute. They cooked up a scheme to pass the cheap stuff
> off as premium **abacá** for Imperial Navy cable and hawser: near-identical dry, **far**
> cheaper, and it rots out in salt water — but they'd be long gone before the fleet's ropes
> failed. They'd pooled the buy-in and were about to feed it into the cable works factory.*
> **Jeff's version:** he ran because he could tell Orkideh was about to have him
> **murdered** and keep the profits. *"It's not me who's the criminal — it's her."*

**GM truth:** *Jeff* stole their pooled assets and bolted, wrecking the venture — and he'll
**never** admit it. Orkideh's version (he ran off with the seed money) is the true one.
Neither can prove a thing; both are guilty of conspiracy to defraud the Empire.

**Why he can't simply be silenced (the leash on Orkideh's player).** Jeff carries a
**contingency** so that killing/losing him doesn't bury the secret — it *releases* it.

### Jeff's contingency — the dead-man's deposit
Jeff has lodged a **packet of fraud documents** with a **merchant in Tel A'bib** — whom he
**refuses to name** (he knows that naming the holder is his death warrant). Standing
instruction: if Jeff does not return to that merchant by a set time, the packet goes to the
**Boetharch of Tel A'bib** — the Vanic imperial authority over the city. The papers lay out
the whole abacá-substitution scheme in detail.

**The twist (let the party walk into it):**
- The documents **also implicate Elmingwed**, and the party will likely seize on that as
  *leverage over their patron.* It isn't. **Elmingwed is wholly untouchable here:** he told
  the Boetharch about the deal the day he set it up. The two share a **standing weekly
  lunch** to compare notes on corrupt practices, handling rivals, and the sundry business of
  running Imperial trade. Confront him with the papers and he'll be politely bored.
- The real exposure is **Orkideh's.** The same set of documents lay out her dealings plainly, 
  and in the Boetharch's hands — *especially* if he learns she's in the area — they spell 
  serious trouble for her. So the controlling move (silence Jeff) is exactly what mails her 
  name to the one official who matters.

*GM levers:* the unnamed merchant is a future investigation (who holds the packet? can it be
bought back, stolen, or intercepted before it reaches the Boetharch?). Jeff will trade
*toward* naming the holder to stay alive, but never actually give it up. See
[[npc:boetharch]] and thread:orkideh-secret.

**Disposition outcomes (→ thread:jeff-fate):**
- **Delivered to Elmingwed** → deal paid, patronage warms — but Jeff talks his way loose
  and returns as an NPC - possibly an enemy who grows in power.
- **Sent back escorted by Jiyloo** → tidy, but the party **loses its muscle** for the
  delve. A real dilemma.
- **Kept along** → a hostile asset in the marching order, watching for a betrayal window.
- **Killed** → the contingency fires; Elmingwed cools.

## Major Scenes

### 1 — The Captive *(social; the heart)*
> *Read-aloud:* "Jeff sits in the dust with his wrists bound, the old tower at his back. He
> works up a grin that doesn't reach his eyes. 'Alright. Before anybody does anything
> they'll regret — let's talk about what I'm worth. And let's talk about Orkideh.'"

The interrogation and bargain. Let the **Orkideh bomb** land here if Jeff judges it'll save
his neck. No die roll settles the he-said/she-said — play it as leverage and reading
people. What's decided here colors everything: whether Jeff guides them, what they believe
about Orkideh, whether the table splits.

### 2 — Approaching Kantaroz
> *Read-aloud:* "The tower is older than anything you've stood near — clearly weathered 
> yet whole and in pristine condition, its pale Bokkaran stone fitted without seam. 
> Four storeys, a hundred feet to the cap."

Stonemasonry/history sense places the stone as ancient **Bokkaran** (same style as the
Sphinx Tomb in the Oasis of Sia). Constructs come and go from a **service hatch** in the
foothills. Knock at the main door and it opens; a **construct butler** offers refreshments
in the waiting hall. The party clocks the **three ways down**.

### 3 — Three ways into the lab
Let the party's choices (and how they've handled Jeff) decide which opens:
1. **Jeff's passphrase.** He learned the Bokkaran door-phrase researching the place. Fast —
   but it means *trusting Jeff* and keeping him close enough to speak it (feeds
   thread:jeff-fate; great wildcard moment if he lies or bolts mid-phrase).
2. **Find the passphrase.** Search/study the four above-ground floors — the phrase is
   written in the **private library** (2nd floor), in Bokkaran. The slow, loot-rich path.
3. **The construct hatch.** Slip in the way the constructs do (force or pick the foothill
   service door). Skips Durel's front door; runs you straight into the working constructs.

### 4 — The Tower Floors *(loot + Durel)*
Four floors, ~100 feet, joined by one stair — but every landing is sealed differently, and
the climb gets harder as you go. Two kinds of barrier: a **hatch** barring the stairhead
(you can see the stair; you just have to get through), and a **hidden stair** whose entrance
must be *found* before you can climb at all.

**Access, floor to floor:**
- **Ground → 1st — a barred hatch** over the stairhead. Simple: force it (Strength) or pick
  it (thieves' tools). Forcing it is *loud* — may draw a construct or Durel himself.
- **1st → 2nd — a hidden stair.** No visible way up. A **secret door** (in Durel's quarters)
  must be found first — Investigation/Perception, or a shortcut from Jeff or Durel's notes.
- **2nd → 3rd — a hatch, locked from above and warded.** A faint Bokkaran glyph trips a
  jolt/alarm if forced carelessly. Pick it patiently, dispel/scratch the glyph, or eat the
  alarm and hurry.

**Ground — reception & public library.**
> *Read-aloud:* "A cool round hall of pale stone. A brass-jointed butler inclines its head
> and offers you water from a tray. Shelves of old books line the curve of the wall; a
> banded stair climbs to a heavy hatch overhead."

The construct **butler** is courteous and harmless unless attacked. The *public* shelves
hold Bokkaran histories — the tower's great age, and (to a careful reader) that the
constructs are **soul-driven**. One passage notes that *"the words that open the deep door
are kept in the master's own library, above"* — pointing at the 2nd-floor passphrase.

**1st — Durel's bedroom & dining.** The likeliest place to **meet Durel** (below). Lived-in
but slipping: a cold half-eaten meal, marginalia in his books turning strange. Hidden here
is the **secret door** to the upward stair — behind the dining hearth, or a shelf that
slides. A modest valuable or two (silver service, a good travel cloak).

**2nd — private library.** The payoff floor: the **lab passphrase** written in Bokkaran
(route 2 into the basement), the **Bokkaran scrolls** (see Rewards), and Durel's **research
journal** — its later pages sliding from real scholarship into the demon's dictation, the
same handwriting going wrong (a chilling handout that foreshadows Scene 5). The warded hatch
to the 3rd floor is here.

**3rd — personal storage.** Durel's private hoard: coin and oddments — and, wrapped in
oilcloth in a cedar case, the one real treasure still here (*Jeff already took the spider
bracelet*):

> **The white-metal dagger (+1).** "A slim dagger of pale, almost luminous metal, its hilt
> worked in fine angular Bokkaran knotwork — plainly the craft of a master, and plainly
> ancient." It is **+1**, but the party only learns that by *using* it (old-school rule — no
> glow, no readout). It's not worth a fortune, yet it's *beautiful*, and that's the catch:
> it's **too fine for any local merchant**, who'll lowball it, assume it's stolen, or simply
> *remember the face* that offered such a thing. **Jiyloo quietly advises against flashing it
> around** Tel A'bib. *(A hook toward a discreet buyer later — or a reason to just keep it.)*

**Durel** plays host, then obstacle. Lucid, he's a courteous scholar; the demon's grip
shows as cold, repeating insistence on "the work." **He's hard to beat head-on** (a
practiced magician backed by constructs). Default outcome at the climax is that the party
**has to put him down**. *Saving* him is the **heroic, hard** path (Scene 5).

### 5 — The Basement *(the apparatus, and the fight)*
> *Read-aloud:* "Cold air, the smell of wet stone and ozone. Channels of black water race
> between the walkways — far too much water for a mountaintop. At the center, three slabs
> of stone hang in the air around a thing that is almost a body: too many joints, breathing
> wetly, not finished. A bank of levers sweats beside it. Durel stands at the levers, lips
> moving to words that aren't his."

The core: the **confinement apparatus** — three floating stone pillars + a **lever control
panel** — holds the half-grown demon and pours the wellhead's power into the summoning.
Durel tends it, dominated.

**The straightforward (and likely) resolution:**
1. **Fight the immature demon body** — the "Caged Whisper" (below). It can't leave the
   pillars, but it hits the minds of anyone close and turns Durel against the party.
2. **Wreck the apparatus** — smash the pillars / throw the levers / cut the water feed. Any
   of these collapses the summoning. **No downstream cost** at this scale — it just ends the
   threat.
3. **Durel** — freed of the apparatus he's usually *already lost*, lashing out; the party
   will **probably have to kill him**.

**The heroic stretch (hard):** reach the man under the strings *before* destroying the
apparatus — get through to him in a lucid flicker, or disrupt the demon's hold without
killing the host (talk him down, restrain him through the demon's panic, time the apparatus
break to free rather than break him). Pulling it off saves Durel — a grateful Bokkaran-magic
contact and a genuine win. *Make them earn it.*

**If they leave it:** a legitimate choice, not a failure. The summoning runs on a **slow
clock** — weeks to months in-world (thread:kantaron-summoning; full staging in *The Kantaron
clock*, Back-to-Tel A'bib branch). Left alone, Durel's constructs range wider and hungrier
for the raw material the ritual needs, and the warning signs climb until the foothills — then
Tel A'bib's eastern approaches — turn unsafe. At the far terminus the body finishes: a full
**Balor** tears loose, lairs at Kantaron, discards Durel, and turns toward Tel A'bib. That
end is held over the table, not sprung cheaply — the party gets many signals, and likely a
paying commission, long before it arrives.

### 6 — Aftermath
Resolve Jeff. Resolve Durel. Update `state.yaml` / `threads.yaml` (see *Post-game seeds*).

## Branch: Back to Tel A'bib (Jeff in tow)
*The party breaks off — after looting the tower, or straight off the capture — and hauls Jeff
back to town to collect from [[npc:elmingwed]]. The basement may be untouched; that's fine
(see **The Kantaron clock**). Run this as a downtime interlude that arms the campaign's next
move rather than a failure state.*

**The spine:** Jeff is a passenger who does not want to arrive. "Joining" the party *was* his
play — a man you've shared a fire with is harder to sell. Every mile he runs the same
negotiation: don't let them hand me to the man I fear. Play the trip and the handoff as one
long con.

### The Kantaron clock (slow)
The summoning is **not** a same-day emergency. It advances on a **slow clock — weeks to months
in-world** (apply the campaign dilation factor; principle 3). What ramps is **the constructs'
hunt for raw material**: completing the demon's body and the ritual needs metal, stone, and
soul-crystal, so Durel's automatons range ever wider and bolder to gather it. That escalation
is the party's early-warning system — and the world's, which advances whether or not they
engage (principle 2).

| Tier | ~In-world time left alone | What the world shows |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | departure | Constructs forage quietly — as the construct **Ren** was first spotted. Nothing in town yet. |
| 1 | ~1–2 weeks | Collection ramps: more machines, ranging lower; a herder or caravan reports constructs hauling off metal and stone. Market rumor. |
| 2 | ~3–6 weeks | Organized stripping — a foothill quarry or hamlet plundered for material; a caravan hit. **Feltan's** ore runs disrupted; merchants grumble; a commission is floated. |
| 3 | ~2–3 months | Constructs reach Tel A'bib's **eastern approaches** — the canon "construct plague" that once rendered the east side impassable. Open civic alarm. |
| 4 | terminus | The body finishes — a full **Balor** breaks loose, lairs at Kantaron, discards Durel, turns toward Tel A'bib (see *Enemies*). |

**Who actually pushes back.** The Imperial detachment under **Renzo** is aloof and
conflict-shy, and Governor **Kilmah Fari** cares nothing for the deep desert — so the *state*
won't deal with it. That vacuum is the hook: by Tier 2–3 the **merchants** pool a bounty.
Likely commissioners:
- **[[npc:elmingwed]]** — your patron; wants the Bokkaran site secured (and its cache for
  himself). The natural, best-paying job, and it warms thread:elmingwed-deal.
- **Feltan** (metalworks; Jiyloo's old master) — constructs stealing ore hit him directly:
  good intel and a smaller, personal commission.
- **Al-Esellin / caravan trade** — the harassed eastern routes.

So leaving Kantaron doesn't drop the thread — it converts it into a **paid return trip** the
party takes on their own clock.

### On the road (Kantaron → Tel A'bib)
- **Jeff the indispensable guide** — knows the water, the shortcut, the safe camp. Each favor
  is a string. Let a PC notice he's *steering* toward a particular gate or shop.
- **Resetting his contingency.** His dead-man's packet sits with an unnamed merchant *in town*
  — so the road home walks him toward his own insurance. He engineers a stop at one specific
  shop "first." Tailing him or reading the tell opens the **who-holds-the-packet?**
  investigation.
- **Wildcard windows** — night watch, a wandering-monster fight, the Inferna Wadi's rickety
  bridges. He won't bolt on bad odds; he banks goodwill. Loose mid-fight, he acts in his own
  interest (the Scene-1 rule, on the trail).
- **The wedge** — *"Why take a finder's fee when I can make you rich? And do you even know who
  you're working for?"* He weaponizes **true** facts about Elmingwed's corruption to turn the
  party off their patron. Lying about himself, honest about Elmingwed — that's why it lands.

### Arriving — the living world has moved
- **News from the east** rides the Kantaron clock above: the longer they dithered, the louder
  the construct rumors greet them at the gate — the dithering tax, delivered as gossip and the
  pull back.
- **Jiyloo's home turf.** Tel A'bib-born, Feltan's former apprentice; she wants the job
  *finished* — Jeff delivered. If the PCs have warmed to him, her pushing the handoff is your
  in-party friction, no monster required.
- **The packet is inside these walls.** Bringing Jeff to town sets him beside his own
  insurance. Surveil him, follow where he *wants* to go, or squeeze him for the name (he trades
  *toward* it, never gives it).

### The handoff to Elmingwed
- **He pays — and that's the trap.** Gracious, settles the pre-bought bounty — but he's an
  **information broker**, not a jailer; he wants to *talk* to Jeff. Per the disposition table,
  **delivered → Jeff talks his way loose and returns as a rival who grows in power**
  (thread:jeff-fate). The party banks coin *and* mints a future enemy who knows the tower, the
  bracelet, and Orkideh's secret. Let them feel both in one scene.
- **What Elmingwed wants from Jeff** may be the **tower's Bokkaran cache**, not a grudge — he's
  the man who flagged the Kantaroz constructs before. That gives Jeff something to sell upward,
  and you a reason the leash slips.
- **The leverage that isn't.** Brandish the fraud docs and he's politely bored —
  [[npc:boetharch]] is his standing weekly lunch. But he *notes* who tried to squeeze him: a
  quiet black mark even as the delivery warms the deal.

### The web tightens — Orkideh & the Boetharch
Tel A'bib is where the **[[npc:boetharch]]** sits, so Orkideh's exposure turns local and
immediate. If Jeff rattles the contingency in town, the Boetharch can extend Orkideh a velvet
"invitation" to that weekly lunch — she gets to *watch* the controlling move mail her own name
in. (Flip thread:orkideh-secret to `active` if it lands.)

### Cashing in the loot (Elmingwed as identifier / fence)
- **The spider bracelet dilemma.** Elmingwed is *the* Bokkaran identifier — covets it, confirms
  ancient + magical, and (old-school rule) won't reveal the [[item:spider-bracelets|Sea-of-Souls
  ward]]. Selling spends your Chekhov's gun; Jeff knows it — *"the bracelet's the real prize,
  not me."*
- **The white-metal dagger (+1), "too fine to sell locally"** — Elmingwed is exactly the
  discreet buyer, for coin or a favor; pays off Jiyloo's keep-it-quiet warning.
- **The crystal-brains with souls inside** — selling bound souls is a faction/moral beat;
  Elmingwed (Athyes faithful) or the temple's **Ennett Wete** will have strong opinions.

### Spin-out threads
- **Kantaron keeps climbing the clock** → the paid return trip.
- **Jeff loose** → recurring rival (thread:jeff-fate).
- **The packet** → a later heist/investigation: buy it back, steal it, or beat it to the
  Boetharch.
- **Patronage warms** → Elmingwed's network opens (Elruen in Bridge City, the militia under
  Abet, scholar contacts).

## Rewards
Hand these out as **described objects, not stat blocks** (world rule —
`lore-and-mysteries.md` → *Item identification & the old-school convention*).

- **The white-metal spider bracelet** *(on Jeff)* — "a heavy bracelet of pale metal,
  wrought as a long-legged spider." Attune and you learn only that it's *magical*. *(It
  wards the Sea of Souls' unseen water — [[item:spider-bracelets]]. Save the reveal for
  when they reach the Sea of Souls: a Chekhov's gun.)*
- **The white-metal dagger (+1)** *(3rd floor)* — beautiful, ancient Bokkaran masterwork;
  modest gold value but **hard to sell locally** (too fine; draws attention). Jiyloo says
  keep it quiet. Full description in Scene 4.
- **Bokkaran scrolls** (2nd-floor library), in Bokkaran — castable only by a reader of
  Bokkaran, or learnable with magic training: *Mending, Encode Thoughts, Alarm* (cantrips);
  *Catapult* (3rd, or 1st for a true sorcerer), *Find Familiar, Blur* (1st).
- **Alchemical & lab supplies**; Bokkaran lore tomes (fuel toward literacy/training).
- **Construct crystal-brains** — each holds an **enslaved soul.** Breaking one kills the
  soul; freeing one needs body + lore the party doesn't have yet. *A reward with a
  conscience attached.*
- **Coin & oddments** (3rd floor); **Elmingwed's payment** if Jeff/his loot is delivered.
- **A freed, grateful Durel** — if they manage the heroic save.
- **Level-up beat:** stopping the summoning and/or resolving Jeff is the milestone to 2.

### Constructs (Durel's worn Bokkaran automatons)
Crystal "brains" hold enslaved souls; Durel commands but can't repair or build them.
Typical: ~2–4 combat constructs + ~3 shop helpers in the shop; workers roaming.

brain:** the glowing crystal is the bound soul that drives it — a called shot that shatters
it (or dropping the construct to 0) stops it instantly; the soul can be *freed* later with
the right body + lore (the party doesn't have it yet). Vulnerable to nothing special — just
sturdy.

(articulated hands can grab, haul, or grapple). Fights mainly to protect itself or other
