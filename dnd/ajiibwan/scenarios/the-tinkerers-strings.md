# The Tinkerer's Strings
### Kantaron Summit — the Kantaroz tower / Tinkerer's Workshop
*A level-1 sandbox adventure for the Deserts of Ajiibwan (game-2 reset).*

> **At the table:** 4 PCs at **level 1**, plus **Jiyloo** (NPC, level 1 Barbarian — the
> front-line muscle) and the captive **Jeff** (level 1 Rogue — a combat wildcard who may
> fight on the wrong side or sit tied up). The GM paces fights by adjusting HP on the fly
> (easy→hard→medium across the session), so the stat blocks below are reference, not gospel
> — don't sweat exact DCs.
>
> Built from the Adventure Template (trimmed). Canon notes: **Durel is alive** here (the
> reanimated "Duroigh" was a prior-run end-state); the **Balor, Nalfeshnee, is incomplete**
> — the party fights an immature body + the summoning apparatus, *not* a CR-19 demon;
> **Maghiel is off-screen.**

**Dramatic question:** *Everyone at Kantaron is on someone's strings — Durel on the
demon's, Jeff on his own lies, Orkideh on her past. Whose strings do you cut, and what
cuts back?*

**The real trap of this adventure:** the summoning fight is **hard**, so the temptation is
to dither — interrogate Jeff, loot the tower, argue — and *not deal with the basement*.
That lollygagging is what lets the demon grow (thread:kantaron-summoning). Dealing with it
promptly costs nothing but a tough fight; **delay is the only thing that makes it worse.**

## Background

Guided by **Jiyloo** (hired by the merchant-magnate **Elmingwed**), the party tracked
**Orkideh's** old criminal partner — **Jeffron Giltor**, "Jeff," a half-orc/half-elf — to
**Kantaron Summit**, and caught him slipping out of an ancient **Bokkaran tower
(Kantaroz)** with loot in his pack. *(That capture has already happened — the session opens
with Jeff trussed and the tower still unentered.)*

The tower is the **Tinkerer's Workshop**. Its sole resident, the artificer-magician
**Durel**, learned to command the site's ancient Bokkaran **constructs** and sends them
into the foothills for materials (the construct **Ren** spotted). Durel thinks he's
recovering lost Bokkaran magic. He's wrong: he has fallen under the **mental control of a
demon** — a Balor, *Nalfeshnee* — being **grown a body and summoned in spirit** in the
basement lab. Durel is the demon's hands now.

Beneath all of it, the basement is a hidden **wellhead of the Sea of Souls**: Bokkaran
leyline magic conjures water there and drains it down the mountain toward Tel A'bib, where
it becomes the unseen water of the Sea of Souls. *(Pure flavor/mystery for this adventure —
disrupting the summoning does **not** harm the Sea of Souls at this scale. It's a seed for
later; see `world/lore-and-mysteries.md`.)*

And in Jeff's pack is the thing Orkideh's deal was really about: a **white-metal Bokkaran
bracelet wrought as a long-legged spider.** *(It's a [[item:spider-bracelets|Sea-of-Souls
ward]] — but per the world's old-school rule, nobody can tell that by looking. See Rewards.)*

## Overall Goal

**No single mandated objective** — this is a sandbox node. The live goals:

- **Settle Jeff** — extract his value (the tower, the bracelet, the way down) and decide
  his fate. *The spine.*
- **Crack Kantaroz** — loot and explore the tower; find the way into the lab.
- **Deal with the summoning** — fight through the immature demon and wreck the apparatus.
  Hard, but the longer they wait, the worse it gets.

## The Captive: Jeff (Jeffron Giltor) — GM dossier
*The most important NPC in the session. A level-1 Rogue. Run him as a survivor, not a
villain.*

**His pitch:** he'd rather throw in with the party than be handed to Elmingwed, whom he
fears. He's useful — he *just looted this tower* and **knows the passphrase to the lab.**
He bargains hard for life and freedom.

**The combat wildcard:** if a fight breaks out and he's loose, he'll act in *his* interest
— stab a distracted PC and bolt, "help" in a way that frees himself, or genuinely fight
beside them if he judges that's his best odds. If he's tied up, the party fights a man down
(no Jeff, and someone may need to mind him). Let that tension ride.

**The bomb he drops (thread:orkideh-secret).** To buy his life he'll tell the party about
him and Orkideh — spun his way:

> *They ran a fiber house — sisal and jute. They cooked up a scheme to pass the cheap stuff
> off as premium **abacá** for Imperial Navy cable and hawser: near-identical dry, **far**
> cheaper, and it rots out in salt water — but they'd be long gone before the fleet's ropes
> failed. They'd pooled the buy-in and were about to seed it into the cable works.*
> **Jeff's version:** he ran because he could tell Orkideh was about to have him
> **murdered** and keep the profits. *"It's not me who's the criminal — it's her."*

**GM truth:** *Jeff* stole their pooled assets and bolted, wrecking the venture — and he'll
**never** admit it. Orkideh's version (he ran off with the seed money) is the true one.
Neither can prove a thing; both are guilty of conspiracy to defraud the Empire.

**Why he can't simply be silenced (the leash on Orkideh's player).** Jeff carries a
**contingency** so that killing/losing him doesn't bury the secret — it *releases* it.

### Jeff's contingency — the dead-man's deposit
Jeff has lodged a **packet of fraud documents** with a **merchant in Tel A'bib** — whom he
**refuses to name** (he knows that naming the holder is his death warrant). Standing
instruction: if Jeff does not return to that merchant by a set time, the packet goes to the
**Boetharch of Tel A'bib** — the Vanic imperial authority over the city. The papers lay out
the whole abacá-substitution scheme in detail.

**The twist (let the party walk into it):**
- The documents **also implicate Elmingwed**, and the party will likely seize on that as
  *leverage over their patron.* It isn't. **Elmingwed is wholly untouchable here:** he told
  the Boetharch about the deal the day he set it up. The two share a **standing weekly
  lunch** to compare notes on corrupt practices, handling rivals, and the sundry business of
  running Imperial trade. Confront him with the papers and he'll be politely bored.
- The real exposure is **Orkideh's.** The same papers document her dealings plainly, and in
  the Boetharch's hands — *especially* if he learns she's in the area — they spell serious
  trouble for her. So the controlling move (silence Jeff) is exactly what mails her name to
  the one official who matters.

*GM levers:* the unnamed merchant is a future investigation (who holds the packet? can it be
bought back, stolen, or intercepted before it reaches the Boetharch?). Jeff will trade
*toward* naming the holder to stay alive, but never actually give it up. See
[[npc:boetharch]] and thread:orkideh-secret.

**Disposition outcomes (→ thread:jeff-fate):**
- **Delivered to Elmingwed** → deal paid, patronage warms — but Jeff talks his way loose
  and returns as a player.
- **Sent back escorted by Jiyloo** → tidy, but the party **loses its muscle** for the
  delve. A real dilemma.
- **Kept along** → a hostile asset in the marching order, watching for a betrayal window.
- **Killed** → the contingency fires; Elmingwed cools.

## Major Scenes

### 1 — The Captive *(social; the heart)*
> *Read-aloud:* "Jeff sits in the dust with his wrists bound, the old tower at his back. He
> works up a grin that doesn't reach his eyes. 'Alright. Before anybody does anything
> they'll regret — let's talk about what I'm worth. And let's talk about Orkideh.'"

The interrogation and bargain. Let the **Orkideh bomb** land here if Jeff judges it'll save
his neck. No die roll settles the he-said/she-said — play it as leverage and reading
people. What's decided here colors everything: whether Jeff guides them, what they believe
about Orkideh, whether the table splits.

### 2 — Approaching Kantaroz
> *Read-aloud:* "The tower is older than anything you've stood near — weathered to the bone
> yet whole, its pale Bokkaran stone fitted without seam. Four storeys, a hundred feet to
> the cap. Down in the scree, a brass-and-bone figure stoops, gathering ore, and trudges
> toward a low hatch in the hillside."

Stonemasonry/history sense places the stone as ancient **Bokkaran** (same style as the
Sphinx Tomb in the Oasis of Sia). Constructs come and go from a **service hatch** in the
foothills. Knock at the main door and it opens; a **construct butler** offers refreshments
in the waiting hall. The party clocks the **three ways down**.

### 3 — Three ways into the lab
Let the party's choices (and how they've handled Jeff) decide which opens:
1. **Jeff's passphrase.** He learned the Bokkaran door-phrase researching the place. Fast —
   but it means *trusting Jeff* and keeping him close enough to speak it (feeds
   thread:jeff-fate; great wildcard moment if he lies or bolts mid-phrase).
2. **Find the passphrase.** Search/study the four above-ground floors — the phrase is
   written in the **private library** (2nd floor), in Bokkaran. The slow, loot-rich path.
3. **The construct hatch.** Slip in the way the constructs do (force or pick the foothill
   service door). Skips Durel's front door; runs you straight into the working constructs.

### 4 — The Tower Floors *(loot + Durel)*
Four levels behind an iron gate, each landing locked:
- **Ground — reception & public library.** The construct butler; shelves of *public*
  Bokkaran texts (history, the tower's age, hints the constructs are soul-driven). A
  patient reader finds rumor of "the words that open the deep door" — pointing upstairs.
- **1st — Durel's bedroom & dining.** The likeliest place to **meet Durel** (see below).
  Personal effects, a half-eaten meal gone cold (he forgets to eat now).
- **2nd — private library.** The good research: the **lab passphrase** (in Bokkaran),
  Durel's notes sliding from scholarship into the demon's dictation, and the **Bokkaran
  scrolls** (see Rewards).
- **3rd — personal storage.** Coin and oddments. *The prize isn't here — **Jeff already
  took the spider bracelet.***

**Durel** plays host, then obstacle. Lucid, he's a courteous scholar; the demon's grip
shows as cold, repeating insistence on "the work." **He's hard to beat head-on** (a
practiced magician backed by constructs). Default outcome at the climax is that the party
**has to put him down**. *Saving* him is the **heroic, hard** path (Scene 5).

### 5 — The Basement *(the apparatus, and the fight)*
> *Read-aloud:* "Cold air, the smell of wet stone and ozone. Channels of black water race
> between the walkways — far too much water for a mountaintop. At the center, three slabs
> of stone hang in the air around a thing that is almost a body: too many joints, breathing
> wetly, not finished. A bank of levers sweats beside it. Durel stands at the levers, lips
> moving to words that aren't his."

The core: the **confinement apparatus** — three floating stone pillars + a **lever control
panel** — holds the half-grown demon and pours the wellhead's power into the summoning.
Durel tends it, dominated.

**The straightforward (and likely) resolution:**
1. **Fight the immature demon body** — the "Caged Whisper" (below). It can't leave the
   pillars, but it hits the minds of anyone close and turns Durel against the party.
2. **Wreck the apparatus** — smash the pillars / throw the levers / cut the water feed. Any
   of these collapses the summoning. **No downstream cost** at this scale — it just ends the
   threat.
3. **Durel** — freed of the apparatus he's usually *already lost*, lashing out; the party
   will **probably have to kill him**.

**The heroic stretch (hard):** reach the man under the strings *before* destroying the
apparatus — get through to him in a lucid flicker, or disrupt the demon's hold without
killing the host (talk him down, restrain him through the demon's panic, time the apparatus
break to free rather than break him). Pulling it off saves Durel — a grateful Bokkaran-magic
contact and a genuine win. *Make them earn it.*

**If they leave it (the trap):** every stretch they spend lollygagging instead of going
down, advance the summoning (thread:kantaron-summoning). Far enough and the body finishes:
a full **Balor** tears loose, lairs at Kantaron, discards Durel, and turns toward Tel A'bib.
That's the threat — held over the table, not sprung cheaply.

### 6 — Aftermath
Resolve Jeff. Resolve Durel. Update `state.yaml` / `threads.yaml` (see *Post-game seeds*).

## Rewards
Hand these out as **described objects, not stat blocks** (world rule —
`lore-and-mysteries.md` → *Item identification & the old-school convention*).

- **The white-metal spider bracelet** *(on Jeff)* — "a heavy bracelet of pale metal,
  wrought as a long-legged spider." Attune and you learn only that it's *magical*. *(It
  wards the Sea of Souls' unseen water — [[item:spider-bracelets]]. Save the reveal for
  when they reach the Sea of Souls: a Chekhov's gun.)*
- **Bokkaran scrolls** (2nd-floor library), in Bokkaran — castable only by a reader of
  Bokkaran, or learnable with magic training: *Mending, Encode Thoughts, Alarm* (cantrips);
  *Catapult* (3rd, or 1st for a true sorcerer), *Find Familiar, Blur* (1st).
- **Alchemical & lab supplies**; Bokkaran lore tomes (fuel toward literacy/training).
- **Construct crystal-brains** — each holds an **enslaved soul.** Breaking one kills the
  soul; freeing one needs body + lore the party doesn't have yet. *A reward with a
  conscience attached.*
- **Coin & oddments** (3rd floor); **Elmingwed's payment** if Jeff/his loot is delivered.
- **A freed, grateful Durel** — if they manage the heroic save.
- **Level-up beat:** stopping the summoning and/or resolving Jeff is the milestone to 2.
