# Gazetteer — Deserts of Ajiibwan

This is the master place-finder for the **Deserts of Ajiibwan** open-world D&D 5e campaign.
It collates every region, settlement, ruin, and notable sublocation drawn from the homebrew
"Ajiibwan Wilderlands" lore, the prior party's play-journal, and the campaign's sited adventure
folders. Each sited location carries the GM's bookkeeping tags in parentheses:
`(#adventure-index, Lx–y level range, HEX coord)`. **The hex coordinates and adventure indices
map directly to the GM's Roll20 hex map** — that map and all battlemaps live in Roll20, not in
this repo (`assets/_raw/` images are reference-only and git-ignored). Use this file to locate a
place, recall what the prior party did there, and find the source doc to read before play.

Notes on sources: "homebrew" docs are the GM's own writeups (filenames given where known);
`Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands` and `Lore - Primary Source materials` are the worldbuilding lore
files; "Journal" is the prior party's session log; "PDF-only" means the folder exists with a
commercial module / Roll20 setup but no homebrew writeup yet. Where a place is catalogued only by
its folder name with no other info, it is marked **catalogued; details in commercial module / Roll20.**

Spellings follow the GM's canonical rulings (`assets/_raw/.extract/canon.md`): **Favarr'n** (gnoll
oasis; formerly Farrav'n/Favvar'n), **Hothme** (not Hoth'ne), **Ukar** (not Ukkar), **Sefurah** (not
Sefura), **El Faiz** (not Al Faiz), **Kantaron** = the summit while **Kantaroz** = the ancient Bokkaran
tower at that summit (both valid). Older forms are kept as parenthetical aliases on first mention.

Many sites carry **two names**: a local **Dornian** name and an official **Vanic** name (e.g. the
**Eye of Kings** [Vanic] = **Cave of Ancestors** [Dornian] are the same complex).

## Tel A'bib & the Near Desert

The home region: the Vanic Imperium's last outpost on the edge of the Great Desert, plus the
ruins, wadis, and oases within a few days' ride. Where the prior party began and the new players
are based.

### Tel A'bib `(home base)`
The last outpost of civilization on the edge of the Ajiibwan (Great) Desert, in the outer reaches
of the Great Empire of Vanic. A walled trading town with a small market, low-end lodging, and land
for purchase; a wadi runs east toward the mountains and feeds it during the rainy season. Home base
for the adventuring collective. Governed by an Imperial Governor (later found complicit in the slave
trade). Now also holds a Sea of Souls dock and boat acquired by the prior party. (In the late Kantaron
arc, construct plague from Kantaron Summit rendered the eastern side impassable and largely abandoned.)
- **New Moon / Moon Tavern** — favorite inn, run by innkeeper Vol, who also keeps party strongbox storage.
- **Market & merchants** — Feltan (metalworks), Al-Esellin (traveling gear/tents), Elmingwed (wealthy merchant and frequent patron).
- **Temple(s)** — including a temple of Athyes; local Taliphan militia; Imperial detachment.
- play_history: Starting city for most party members and primary hub of operations throughout the
  whole campaign — caravan investigations, the missing-child rescue, archaeological work, rest/resupply.
  Prior party later discovered the Governor's slave-trade complicity, and acquired a Sea of Souls boat and dock here.
- status: active — party stronghold
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Journal

### Inferna Wadi
A wadi (dry riverbed) flowing east from Tel A'bib toward the mountains; the town's rainy-season
water source, completely dry and passable in the dry season, crossed by rickety unmaintained bridges.
(Note: "Inferna Wadi" is also the name of an antiquities district inside the Holy Cities — see that region.)
- **The Wadi / clay valley** — the dry channel holds clay deposits worked by local children for pottery; site of the boy Raffin's kidnapping and a recent excavation.
- play_history: Party tracked the missing boy Raffin here (Day 5), found a clay excavation site and drag-marks leading toward the mountains and the Ancient Tower; camped and were woken by an earthquake.
- status: active (crime scene, monitored)
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Journal

### Eye of Kings — Royal Tomb `(#9, L1, hex 1312)`
A royal tomb / cave complex outside Tel A'bib, reached through a narrow-walled valley. Local young
men traditionally enter the caves and run out quickly to prove their valor. Filled with undead and
traps; currently held by the Cult of Anauld (a sect of the Church of Dorne). Contains skeleton
guardians, an invisible-water chamber (psychically damaging), and a stone centipede.
**Naming:** "Eye of Kings" is the **Vanic** official name; **Cave of Ancestors** is the local
**Dornian** name for the SAME complex (see "Caves of Ancestors / Tomb of Ancestors" below, which is
this site under its Dornian name).
- play_history: Essen's brother Tane and two friends vanished attempting the rite; party entered (Day 6, the Anauld's Eve incident), cleared skeleton guardians, rescued the children alive, and recovered a metal bracelet with a spider design. Cleared of immediate threats but still hazardous.
- status: dangerous (supernatural hazards remain)
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Journal

### Kantaron — Tinkerer's Workshop `(#1, L3–6, hex 1810)` First Encounter
A 120-ft ancient tower in exceptional condition in the mountains northeast of Tel A'bib, near the
wadi. Library on the second floor, bedroom on the third, basement with water features; a door that
opens by itself; apparently construct/magical in nature. Purpose unclear. (Distinct from the Air Spire tower.)
- play_history: Found while tracking the missing boy Raffin (Day 5). Party explored all floors, fought a hostile animated-chest construct, found no clear inhabitants or artifacts. Appears uninhabited.
- status: explored; purpose unclear
- sources: Journal

### Kantaron — Tinkerer's Workshop `(#1, L3–6, hex 1810)` Second Encounter
The high mountain **summit (Kantaron)** in the eastern region near Tel A'bib, crowned by **Kantaroz** —
an ancient ~120-ft Bokkaran tower built at the summit millennia ago, in excellent condition. Beneath the tower lies the artificer
Duroigh's (Durel's) magical **Tinkerer's Workshop** — engine room, living quarters, workshop with a
mind-enslaving lab and demon-assembly area, and ventilation/access systems, all powered by Bokkaran
technology. The tower has four gated/locked levels (engine room, living quarters, workshop, parapet).
The artificer was entranced and attempting to bring a demon (a Nalfeshnee) into the world; surrounding
foothills hold construct material-gathering sites.
- play_history: Major adventure arc — party defeated the demon and took possession of the workshop. Later "Crisis at Kantaron Summit" and "Pursuing Nalfeshnee" arcs followed; the construct plague from here damaged Tel A'bib's eastern side and drying of the Sea of Souls drove a pursuit arc.
- status: under party control; dangerous, magical
- sources: Tinkerer's Workshop: Kantaroz Summit.md; Crisis at Kantaron Summit.md; Pursuing Nalfeshnee at the Kantaron Summit.md; Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

### Air Spire `(#2)`
An ancient tower at the shore of the Great Oasis, paired with **The Talisman** — a spherical
teleportation device hovering ~800 ft above the oasis center, reached by a rickety floating gondola
(round trip ~50–60 min). The tower has four levels (engine room, living quarters, workshop, parapet),
is guarded by two mated wyverns, and contains a "flesh blob" (a former level-10 wizard).
- play_history: — (no recorded prior-party visit)
- status: active / dangerous
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

### Great Oasis
A major oasis south/southeast of Tel A'bib in the central desert, with multiple encampments and
temporary settlements; the Air Spire and its Talisman stand at its shore. ; boar herds roam it.
- play_history: Travel landmark and staging point. Party encountered a boar herd (Day 10) and planned to plant storm-orchid seeds here (Day 24, per the Lillith Nomads deal).
- status: active
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Journal

### Dimark `(L3–4, hex 1210)`
A small village northwest of Tel A'bib in the "Black Smoke" area. Villagers use a Bokkaran spell
engine to gain spellcasting ability, and a protective totem against Bokkaran magical curses. The path
to its temple is guarded by illusions and guardian creatures.
- **Glade of Tanil** — a nearby sacred grove (Black Smoke area), created by Ukar, guarded by a 20–25-ft wooden-club guardian; holds a Tanil temple with a bloodstained altar, dryads, and floating lights; the path conjures visions of the visitors' past trauma.
- play_history: Party visited with the guide Serinde (Day 26); fought the guardian and a summoned wolf, met dryads, learned Tanil's tenets, and several members (A''ki, Kai, Silverin) converted to Tanil worship; accessed the temple of Tanil.
- status: active; party-friendly
- sources: Journal

### Black Smoke and Tar
A hazardous desert area northwest of Tel A'bib with noxious black smoke and tar; contains Dimark and
the Glade of Tanil. Associated with Bokkaran magical curses and protective totems.
- play_history: Traversed en route to Dimark (Day 26).
- status: active (hazardous terrain)
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Journal

### Cave of Ancestors / Tomb of Ancestors (= Eye of Kings)
An ancient cave/tomb system outside Tel A'bib with undead inhabitants and magical wards. Closely
associated in the journal with the Eye of Kings rescue (children playing near it). **Canon:** this is
the **same complex** as the Eye of Kings — **Cave of Ancestors** is the local **Dornian** name,
**Eye of Kings** the official **Vanic** name (`#9, L1, hex 1312`). The two entries are kept because
the journal uses both names; treat them as one site.
- play_history: Children were playing in/near the tomb (Day 3); cleared of the child threat during the Day 6 rescue.
- status: cleared of immediate threat; hazardous
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Journal

## The Deep Desert & Oases

The broad central desert between Tel A'bib and the southern/western cities — the Blistering Dunes,
the major oases and caravan routes, the cut-off river cities of the south, and the scattered cities
and ruins in between. Many here are catalogued by folder only (PDF-only / module).

### Blistering Dunes
A dangerous central-desert region; its central passage connects trade routes via Favarr'n and the
Oasis of Sia. Several caravans have gone missing on this route.
- play_history: Caravan-disappearance investigation mission originated from here.
- status: active / dangerous
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

### Oasis of Sia `(#16, L2–4, hex 1213)`
A major, ancient oasis on the central passage through the Blistering Dunes, southwest of Tel A'bib.
Holds ruined settlements and temples — including a **Temple of Dorne** whose religious artifacts had
been looted — and an ancient **Sphinx** with subterranean levels. The Black Water Cult used it for
excavations. (Note: folder index #16; the lore also references an "ONA 15" commercial-module mapping.)
- **The Sphinx (Oasis of Sia)** — an ancient sphinx (a different style from the Djeteke sphinx) with chambers, guard statues / living statues, prayer halls, and a massive treasure chamber behind a secret door; multiple subterranean levels, magical wards, and ancient traps.
- play_history: Investigated repeatedly (Days 1–3, 8–9). On Day 8 the party (with Elmingwed's soldiers and local Taliphan militia) assaulted the Black Water Cult camp, burned tents, fought cultists, and explored the sphinx; recovered an enormous treasure hoard (ancient coins — taboo in Tel A'bib — sealed crates, an ornate throne, weapons, coptic jars) and the Wand of Identification "Inventia," sold artifacts to Elmingwed (40% deal). Oasis reopened to trade after the cult was driven out.
- status: cleared of Black Water Cult; partially looted; artifacts remain
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Journal

### Djeteke — Jarab `(#25, hex 1317)`

### B'rath `(#13, hex 1412)`
A desert village in the southern region near the Sea of Souls, also called the "Village of Fire
Slugs / Fire Beetles." Its inhabitants are children and families who harvest fire-beetle oil in nearby
caves as a coming-of-age ritual; all B'rathians speak Ukar fluently (taught by the titan Mesos). The
village welcomes the party and refills fire-beetle-oil lanterns for a fee. Connected to Mesos'
underground prison. **Mesos** — the imprisoned Titan King — is the *near*, relatively easy-to-find
titan here near Tel A'bib / B'rath, **distinct from Afra**, the slumbering titan in her own Ukar
complex far to the **west of Hothme** (see The Granite Empire). (See **The Sea of Souls & Subterranean** for the B'rath Caves / Mesos complex.)
- play_history: A titan's-finger artifact responded to B'rath's proximity; party traveled here, met the children, descended into the caves, and witnessed coming-of-age ordeals.
- status: established ally; cultural hub; entry point to the titan caves
- sources: The Titan's Finger and the Fire Beetles Adventure.md; Return to Mesos.md; Freeing Mesos.md; Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

## The Saragubi Desert

The vast southern desert region, home of the Tugari and gnolls, riddled with Bokkaran ruins and a
focus of Black Water Cult operations. Reached from Hothme across an ancient bridge over the Saragubi
Rift; the major sited adventure here is the Pyramid of the Lost King complex.

### Saragubi Desert & Pyramid of the Lost King `(#19, L1–5, hex 0515)`
A vast desert region of southern Ajiibwan, home to the Tugari and to gnoll tribes (the Rrave tribe
runs an oasis). Holds Black Water Cult strongholds, giant sandworms, scorpions, bulettes, and many
Bokkaran ruins and water sources. Accessible from Hothme via an ancient bridge over the Saragubi Rift.
The **Pyramid of the Lost King** is its great Bokkaran landmark — its obvious entrances long looted,
serving mainly as a navigation point; the actual dungeon (the Hidden Crypt) lies nearby.
- **The Disk** — a large Bokkaran portal/transport artifact ringed by ancient Bokkaran bones, inscribed with Bokkaran mottos ("Proudly into darkness," "Through hardship, no one is beyond reach," etc.); magical output fluctuates with the wind. Possible alternate route to Saragubi sites.
- **Whistling Towers** — an irregular field of ~40-ft rough-hewn stone towers ~50 ft apart that whistle in the wind; a magical engine. Casters can recover spell slots (6 hrs + DC14 Arcana) and a DC12 Performance harmonizing with them can grant True Seeing for 24 hrs.
- **Crypt of the Saragubi Desert** (Hidden Crypt / Bokkaran Wizard King's Crypt) — a vast underground tomb near the Pyramid: Warrior's Altar, Tanil's Sanctuary, Tomb of the Bokkaran Undead King **Donol** (white-metal/gold sarcophagus), Secret Crypt, Arcane Altar, bridge crossing, Altar of Lore, secret treasure room. Reached via the Sea of Souls; guarded by undead soldiers and spirits (Indith, Imroth); collapses after Donol's death (escape via a boulder-trap gauntlet).
- **Kalinore's Domain** (Kalinore's Tomb/Temple) — a sealed palace-temple complex in a southeastern canyon; entrances blocked by salt pillars opened via music boxes. Includes **Ilymna's Palace** (musical/performance magic) and **Kalinore's Temple** (death/ritual magic); inner Kalinore's Caverns need both music boxes synchronized. Stone golems, ghostly guardians, a consequence/benefit magical pool, libraries, ritual chambers.
- **Charnel Keep** — a massive Bokkaran castle-tomb (above-ground castle + vast underground undead tombs). Castle rooms include a chess hall with ghostly players, a poisoned-feast dining room, a trophy room of undead taxidermy, a cursed library, a possessed armory, a flesh/bone-golem laboratory, a mummy crypt, and a shrine guarded by spectral knights around an obsidian statue of Malleus. Underground: a worker's area with a Sea of Souls waterfall/river, a giant-spider lair, ritual temples, a Bokkaran power plant. Occupied by **Kalinore** (generating magical storms) and **Maghiel** (undead research). Holds the anteroom Ritual of Ancient Light and Authority, a Hall of Guardians, and a Prismatic Crystal control mechanism; engineering workshop under **Galim**.
- **Gnoll Village of Gila** — a non-hostile gnoll settlement; provided the party a (damaged) trading boat in exchange for killing Black Water Cult knights.
- **Black Water Cult Compound** — a fortified canyon compound with ritual training areas (Maghiel teaches spellcasting/undead control), defended by cultist guards and Black Water Knights; a waypoint for supplies to the Tugari and cultists.
- **Saragubi Rift / Rift Bridge** — the great chasm separating Saragubi from main Ajiibwan; its ancient bridge is the primary crossing. Destroyed during Kalinore's escape, cutting off supply and travel and forcing Tugari route negotiations.
- **Basq** — a Vanic Imperium outpost town near the Rift Bridge, damaged by magical attacks after Kalinore's release; survivors need aid; neglected by the Vanic Governor, supplied by an m'Thorsz caravan.
- **Tugari Lands** — Tugari tribal territory; leader Tenetu; outriders lead giant worms away from caravans. The party passed through during a caravan delivery, met Tenetu and Weshkhasi, fought ritual combat, won Tugari cooperation, and gained the minder/escort Onsun.
- play_history: Central setting for multiple arcs (Days 25, 27, 40, 43–48, 84–85). The party found the Pyramid as a landmark after exiting a Sea of Souls crypt (Day 46); explored the Hidden Crypt (recovering major treasure and surviving Donol); ran the Kalinore's Tomb arc (dual music-box mechanics; Kalinore escaped via a magical bonfire; the Rift Bridge was destroyed in the escape); ran the Charnel Keep / "Kalinore's Chaos" arc and the Basq aftermath relief caravan; allied with gnolls and Tugari against the Black Water Cult. Also see the **Nurian Necropolis** (below), explored Days 84–85.
- status: active, dangerous; partially explored; Rift Bridge destroyed (being repaired)
- sources: Saragubi adventure documents — Navigating the Saragubi Desert.md; Finding the Crypt of the Saragubi Desert.md; Escaping the Crypt of the Saragubi Desert.md; Kalinore's Tomb adventure.md; Kalinore's Chaos in the Saragubi Desert.md; Basq - Kalinore's Aftermath.md; Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Lore - Primary Source materials; Journal

## The Granite Empire

The capital and major cities of the Granite Empire are likely out of scope for this world document and future adventures.

A powerful, isolationist empire west of the Ajiibwan Desert across the Red Plains. It bars
Tolorian/Vanic travelers from its borders (swift justice for attempts) but trades through desert
peoples, and holds an ancient pact with the Tugari. Hothme is a border city rules by the dwarf monarch m'Thorsz; Hothme is a client city to the Granite Empire, and survives by balancing powers between merchants and the two Empires, where no one benefits by overthrowing him: this unstable situation makes him both paranoid and hungry for information and additional sources of control or power. Deeper within the Granite Empire is the hidden world tree **El Faiz** (formerly "Al Faiz"); **west of Hothme** lies **Afra's complex** — the ruined Ukar city/ruin cluster around the slumbering titan Afra and the entry to the Ukar tree-portal network.

### Hothme `(hex 0117)` — Granite Empire capital
The Granite Empire's trading "desert port" and powerful city-state, the "Gateway Desert City" on the Red Plains
border, roughly halfway between the Vanic and Granite Empires; the prior party's primary home base in
the later campaign. Ruled by the **m'Thorsz** (the dwarf monarch / Dhallak Carn). A major trade hub
with Sea of Souls port access, multiple guilds, scholarly institutions, thieves' networks, and host
merchant houses (Cascabel/Casobel, Gideon/Clan Gideon, Vedek, Zingolfin, Golden Camel).
- **Palace of Thorsz / throne room** — the m'Thorsz's seat and secured chambers; site of party briefings.
- **Grand Forum** — the major civic plaza, containing the Sacred Pool and the Casting Guild portal area. (Damaged in Maghiel's fireball attack.)
- **Sacred Pool** — a public pool in the Grand Forum; site of Maghiel's fireball attack with civilian casualties, which triggered m'Thorsz's investigation of the party.
- **Valeska's Needle / Veleska's Needle** — a tower of Bokkaran-style architecture housing the Casting Guild and the elf wizard-scholar **Burga** (with Dolasea and Irrissee); extensive Bokkaran lore; where Kalinore's music box was maintained; start of the Kalinore's Tomb arc.
- **Gate of Scorpions** — the overland entrance.
- **Red Unicorn tavern**; markets and shops.
- **Party Residence** — a large house given to the party in a non-prestigious quarter, staffed by household manager Ritabu Loklir and hired guards; their Hothme base.
- play_history: The party traveled here via the Sea of Souls (Day 43), fought a purple wyrm/worm at the dock with dwarven allies, stayed with Clan Gideon, toured the city and learned its politics, located a crypt and a Sea of Souls outlet (Days 44–46); used Hothme as a sanctuary and diplomatic hub thereafter, negotiating with m'Thorsz between adventures.
- status: active major city; friendly to party / home base
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; all Saragubi adventure documents; The Dark Arts of Diplomacy: The Holy Cities.md; Freeing Mesos.md; Journal

### El Faiz & Sefurah Springs
The Granite Empire's deep, hidden sacred oasis, marked by a peculiar rock formation resembling a giant
weeping willow. Now dormant and barren, it once flourished with lush foliage and an ethereal river. At
its center stands the **El Faiz** tree (formerly "Al Faiz"; the "world tree" / "Wind's Sorrow") — a
gargantuan weeping willow with white bark and quicksilver leaves that heal wounds, soothe fevers, and
cure toxins; bonded to the Titans, it changes leaf color with Titan emotion and can be reawakened by
the Hymn of Harmony (the Roshanai [formerly Roshani] wreath + Parvaiz bird ritual). **Sefurah Springs**
(formerly Sefura Springs) are the magical springs that feed the tree, powered by Storm Orchids that
drive enhancements and precipitation.
- **God King's Mirror** — the entrance to the material path to the El Faiz tree (its material-plane manifestation).
- **Battle to Enter Sefurah / Twin Altars / Scarred Jungle / Final Conflict** — the staged sublocations of the El Faiz & Sefurah Springs adventure folder (sited; details in the homebrew/module docs).
- play_history: Mesos described the tree's history, location, and awakening ritual (Day 81); the prior party pursued the world-tree restoration quest into the Granite Empire (Days 80+), including the Sefurah Springs approach.
- status: lost/dormant location; strategic to world-tree restoration; sited adventure arc
- sources: Lore - Primary Source materials; Return to Mesos.md; Journal

### Afra's Complex — the Slumbering Titan `(west of Hothme)`
A ruined **Ukar** complex on the Granite Empire side of the desert, **west of Hothme** (folder:
"Slumbering Titan"). Home of the dormant titan **Afra**, "the Slumbering Titan." **Very hard to find;
a late-game site.** Afra and **Mesos are distinct titans** — Afra is this hard-to-find *western* one
(west of Hothme), while Mesos is the imprisoned Titan King near **Tel A'bib / B'rath** (see B'rath and
the Sea of Souls section). Approached correctly, Afra divulges deep lore and opens the **Ukar "tree
portal network"** — the Ukar equivalent of the Sea of Souls: a tree-based teleportation network she
herself developed, with portals throughout the world. Operating a portal requires **Afra's Spark**
physically incorporated into a creature's body.

## The Holy Cities (Bluffside)

A great inland Vanic Empire city complex of religious, military, trade, and political power, modified
in play as "Bluffside" (formerly "Sem La Vah"). Reached via the Sea of Souls through the Sem La Vah
underground docks. The focus of the prior party's diplomatic mission and a Black Water Cult
infiltration plot (kidnapping, well-poisoning).

### Holy Cities (Bluffside) `(#6, hex 0822)`
An ancient city complex of five-plus primary districts, a center of religious, military, trade, and
political power, accessible via the Sea of Souls through the Sem La Vah docks. Politically volatile and
partially under Black Water Cult influence; water supply controlled by a leader styled "Thorsz." (Lore
also lists the elite-district name **Ell Bastis** as the journal's name for the whole urban center.)
- **Sem La Vah** — secret underground docks and trading community beneath the Holy Cities, reached via the Sea of Souls; cavernous, with vessels, makeshift piers, and secretive restricted trade (entry by letters of introduction). Met the Dockmaster and contact Tal El-Mazari.
- **Sheloah** — the dwarven mining district; industrial yet finely built, with crystalline magical ore, Clan Gideon's trading house, the hotel **Khrumai's Rest** (the party's base, named for the dwarf hero Khrumai Forgehammer), and the Black Water Cult HQ beneath the streets.
- **Nabi Nageab** — the old-town/market district; holds **Ashburne Academy** and the legendary **Black Lion Tavern**; where cultists extort merchants. (Party spotted and captured the cultist "Dil Logoin" here, located Nassa Yahya's gallery.)
- **Nabi Nagiab** — the temple/shrine district; the High Sun Temple and the Temple of Silent Wind.
- **Zakutar** — the military district; dark-stone fortress, barracks, training grounds, under General Koshara, Captain Allisan, and Master-at-Arms Rishka.
- **Ell Bastis** — the wealthy elite/new-city district; ornate mansions, gardens; high society (Begum Fatima Zaman, Count Iskandar Khiray, Nassa Yahya, etc.); the Black Water Cult runs a clandestine compound here; extensive sewers connect the districts.
- **Inferna Wadi (district)** — the antiquities and desert-lore scholarly district; Nassa Yahya's gallery, Dr. Isilla Rissan's gallery (with a Ukar portal), the merchant Elseron the Unseen, and Soraya Fawaz's orphanages (a cult cover).
- **Birjari / Birjanl** — an administrative/merchant residential district (home of bard Aila Yaşar and merchant Youssef al-Salim).
- play_history: Party traveled here via the Sea of Souls on m'Thorsz's diplomatic mission (Days 70–73), lodged at Khrumai's Rest, attended the Holy City Ball/gala (met Count Iskandar Khiray, Nassa Yahya, and others), visited the antiquities district (Day 72) for artifact sales and intelligence, captured a cultist, and infiltrated the Black Water Cult compound through the sewers (Day 73), uncovering a well-poisoning conspiracy and fighting a purple worm in a captive-animal area.
- status: active; politically volatile; partially under cult influence
- sources: The Dark Arts of Diplomacy: The Holy Cities.md; The Dark Arts: Caravan Rescue.md; The Dark Arts: Raid the Cult's Desert Fort.md; Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Lore - Primary Source materials; Journal

### Black Water Cult Desert Fort
A fortified mesa base built into a desert rock formation inland from the Holy Cities. Rooms include
aerie lookouts, a stockroom, a smithy (with the imprisoned dwarf Rochig in an explosives room), a

- play_history: Party scouted, infiltrated, and assaulted the fort during the Caravan Rescue / Raid arc, rescued Rochig, recovered artifacts and documents, and negotiated control with Na'ilah Kopak and the Holy Cities government (may retain or hand over).
- status: active cult stronghold; under party assault; potential party asset
- sources: The Dark Arts: Raid the Cult's Desert Fort.md; The Dark Arts: Caravan Rescue.md; Journal

## The Sea of Souls & Subterranean

The magical underground sea of invisible water beneath the Great Desert, and the subterranean sites
reached through it — B'rath's titan caves, the lost city of Bokkaral, the Crystal Towers, and the
hidden Bokkaran docks. The prior party's principal late-campaign travel network.

### Sea of Souls
A vast magical underground sea of invisible ("Unseen") water beneath the Great Desert, spanning from
the Tel A'bib region to the Granite Empire and beyond. Only boats of ancient timber can cross it;
untrained travelers fall asleep unless warded or magically immune; the invisible water is psychically
damaging to touch unprotected and glows when magical creatures move through it. Home to many creatures
(scorpion swarms, undead, glowing fish) and Bokkaran architecture and monuments. Multiple ports and
outlets connect surface sites; some entrances are abandoned, others guarded by towns or creatures. An
alternate trade route to distant lands (Granite Empire, drow territories). The party holds a magical
sailboat (from m'Thorsz) and a dock at Tel A'bib; Thieri/Thieri developed a 72-hour access potion.
- **Tel A'bib Dock** — the party's Sea of Souls access point at Tel A'bib (boat + dock); contact Jiyloo; nearby caverns explored.
- **Sem La Vah** — Holy Cities underground docks (see Holy Cities region).
- **Mesos' Dock** — a hidden Bokkaran dock-and-warehouse complex (coordinates from Bokkaran), infested with undead ghouls and spirits; an inner warehouse with a Guardian's Fire (3d8 lightning) protecting a stone box, an Alchemist's Lab, an Echoing Murals hall (learn Guardian's Fire mechanics, Arcana DC16), Bokkaran runes (DC14), a Scribe's Overlook (Scroll of Amplify Gravity), and a Dormitory (Ebony Nightshade Pendant, Starlight Sand Timer). Party cleared it and recovered the Dream Catcher artifact.
- **Mesos' prison cavern** — an underground cavern-prison holding the titan Mesos, reached via the Sea of Souls; Mesos taught the party Ukar ideology and lore of El Faiz and the Roshanai wreath / Parvaiz bird (Day 81).
- play_history: Primary late-campaign travel method (Days 44–46, 81–82, 91+). Used to reach the Saragubi crypt, Hothme, the Holy Cities, B'rath, and Mesos' Dock; encountered glowing fish, fog banks, monsters, and ancient structures; the sea dried up due to Nalfeshnee damage at Kantaron, triggering a pursuit/restoration arc, and was restored by a Bokkaran magical engine.
- status: active travel/exploration hub; partially mapped
- sources: Mesos' Dock.md; Sea of Souls Finding a Trade Route adventure.md; Caverns near the Tel A'bib Dock: Sea of Souls Adventure.md; Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Journal; and other Sea of Souls documents

### B'rath Caves & Mesos' Prison Complex
The extensive cavern system beneath the village of B'rath (the fire-beetle caves), doubling as the
titan Mesos' prison. Charted areas: (1) children's antechamber/camp; (2) a deep roped descent;
(3) a fork to a spider lair or down stairs; (4) a Bokkaran Artificer's Workshop (tools, an iron golem
guardian, an animated-tool swarm, elemental teleport-trap glyphs, the recoverable Bokkaran Lathe);
(5) a Bokkaran Dream Trap room (fire beetles and larvae, ~944 dream spikes, dead creatures); (6) a
control room with the crippled titan hand (6 ft, only thumb and pinkie remaining); (7) a geothermally
heated mud bath (a Bokkaran scrying countermeasure); (8) Mesos' mud pool (the submerged, incomplete
titan). Spiders (Crag Spiders, a Fiend Spider) and fire beetles inhabit it.
- play_history: Party descended with the children as guides, fought spiders, explored the artificer's workshop, studied/disabled the Dream Trap, rejoined the titan finger to the crippled hand, and met Mesos; returned multiple times (Return to Mesos, Freeing Mesos) for progressively deeper discoveries and to free the titan.
- status: ongoing exploration; partially charted; major lore/artifact source
- sources: The Titan's Finger and the Fire Beetles Adventure.md; Return to Mesos.md; Freeing Mesos.md

## Other Sited Ruins & Sites

Sited adventure locations that don't sit cleanly in one region above (mostly the Shunned-world arc and
the southern river cities), plus large background polities.

### Bridged City — Scholarly Schism `(#11, Shunned #2, L2–4)`
A major city, southwest of Tel A'bib (through Eremas, beyond the Haunting Range), built against a
mountain and home to the **University of Antiquities and Engineering** (also called the College of
Magic and Engineering, led by Savila). Its scholars specialize in Desert Script and Bokkaran study,
are extremely intelligent (do not try to outsmart them — Elmingwed's emphatic advice), and have a
working relationship with Tsendur. Entry requires an introduction. Cut off from the Empire by the
Dangerous Road and a river blockade; follows Durgan's Rest in the Shunned campaign sequence. The elven
magician Elruen and the lizard-man Yormon Therdasiroth operate from here.
- **Haunting Range** — the mountain range barrier west/southwest of Tel A'bib that complicates access to the Bridged City.
- play_history: Referenced as an artifact-research destination (Day 5); later party members attended College of Magic studies and interacted with graduate students (Days 15+); a College tower hosted Maghiel's apparition.
- status: active scholarly center; somewhat hostile to outsiders; isolated
- sources: Journal; Mesos' Dock.md; Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; commercial module (Crypts of Azarumme / Shadows of Lastwatch Keep)

### Durgan's Rest — Mishap of Ill Portent `(#27, Shunned #1, L1–3, hex 2012)`
A mountain town (also "Ordurt") in the foothills northeast of the Great Oasis; precedes the Bridged
City in the Shunned campaign sequence. **Tsendur**'s homestead is here (a half-orc magician and
Bokkaran-language authority); also a watering hole, meat-prep and taxidermy businesses, and a community
of scholars and merchants (the dwarf Kada among them). An orc resident is known to Elmingwed. Attacked
by dark elves and fire beings during the prior campaign.
- play_history: Party traveled here for Bokkaran-language consultation with Tsendur (Days 10–14), traded a Bokkard book with kobolds en route, met Kada and other dwarves, and was attacked by Maghiel's forces during a night watch. Dain Ironheart later departed here on business with Tsendur. (Note: the sited "Mishap of Ill Portent" adventure itself is PDF-only / not yet written up.)
- status: active; some hostility from dark-elf threats
- sources: Journal; The Titan's Finger and the Fire Beetles Adventure.md; Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; PDF-only (adventure module)

### Covent of the Weeping Moon `(L4, hex 2312)`
A marshy, haunted area on the **east side of the mountains** that bracket the Ajiibwan Desert —

earlier lore conflation "Weeping Moon = Chandler's Folly (one place)" is **incorrect**. The Covent and
the Radiant Necropolis are **separate, adjacent** sites — **northeast of the Covent** lies the

### Chandler's Folly — Radiant Necropolis `(L4–6, hex 2510)`
A town reachable by boat up a river (and overland to Lapo), connected to ruins and to **Sideraeon** (a
Bokkaran magical power source). The prior party used it for rest and recovery and as a staging point.
The sited adventure is the **Radiant Necropolis** — an **ancient Titan tomb**, lying **northeast of
the Covent of the Weeping Moon** (hex 2312; see previous entry). **Canon:** the Radiant Necropolis and
the Covent are **distinct, neighboring** sites — disregard the old lore that merged "Weeping Moon" into
Chandler's Folly as a single place.
- play_history: Referenced as a travel destination and staging point (Day 15); the party discussed routes and used it for expeditions and rest.
- status: active town; party-friendly (Radiant Necropolis adventure not yet written up)
- sources: Journal; PDF-only (Radiant Necropolis module); Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

### Granite Empire (polity)
The powerful empire west of the Ajiibwan Desert (see its region section above for sited locations). It
bars Tolorian/Vanic entry (swift justice for attempts), trades through desert peoples, and holds an
ancient pact with the Tugari Homeland. Ruled from Hothme; contains El Faiz and (west of Hothme) Afra's complex.
- play_history: The prior party explored Granite Empire regions extensively in the later campaign (Days 80+), especially Saragubi and toward El Faiz.
- status: active; isolationist / can be hostile to Vanic travelers
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Journal

### Vanic Imperium (polity)
The large conglomerate of states east of the Ajiibwan Desert (similar to ancient Rome), ruled by a
central authority; all player characters originate from somewhere in the Vanic states, where the common
tongue is Vanicul. Tel A'bib is its last outpost on the edge of the Great Desert.
- play_history: The party's origin nation.
- status: active
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands
