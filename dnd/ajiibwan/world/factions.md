# Factions & Peoples of Ajiibwan

A compendium of the powers, peoples, cults, and guilds of the Deserts of Ajiibwan — the empires that rule it, the ancient civilizations whose ruins and magic still shape it, the cults that menace it, and the merchant houses and guilds that run its cities.

---

## Powers & Nations

**Vanic Imperium** (also *Vanic Empire*, "the Empire," "Vanic power") — The dominant imperial political power of the settled regions, controlling the Holy Cities and surrounding territories as well as outlying areas like Basq. Comprised of multiple districts with competing factions (military, merchant, religious, political), governed through a Senate alongside an imperial family (the Vanic Emperor is a distant relation of Mirza Hadrian Andruli) and a military hierarchy that includes General Koshara. Local administration runs through governors (e.g., the imperial governor at Basq). The Imperium is the origin culture of all player characters (see *Tolorian peoples*).
- Goals / character: rule through a mix of military force and political intrigue; prioritize trade and bureaucratic order. Frequently indifferent to "distractions" like fighting constructs at Kantaron Summit, and slow to provide aid (e.g., after the Basq bridge destruction).
- Relationship to the party/world: the primary political stage for the Holy Cities arc; a partial ally. Currently destabilized by political unrest and Black Water Cult infiltration. The party may present evidence to the Senate and help prevent a Cult coup or political collapse.
- Key figures: General Koshara; the Vanic Emperor; the Basq imperial governor; see *Mirza Hadrian Andruli*.
- Status: active, bureaucratic; under threat from internal infiltration.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Crisis at Kantaron Summit.md; Basq - Kalinore's Aftermath.md; The Dark Arts of Diplomacy: The Holy Cities.md; The Dark Arts: Caravan Rescue.md; The Dark Arts: Raid the Cult's Desert Fort.md

**Granite Empire** (also "Granite power," "m'Thorsz's realm") — Dwarven-ruled empire seated at Hothme (capital), spanning inland mountain and desert regions, led by **m'Thorsz**. Maintains Sea of Souls sailing capability (including a magical sailboat).
- Goals / character: pursue trade partnerships and political influence; prevent Vanic Imperium destabilization and keep trade routes open. Interested in controlling the captured desert fort and Sea of Souls access.
- Relationship to the party/world: a patron faction and direct party supporter. m'Thorsz dispatches the party to the Holy Cities and provides resources, letters of introduction, the magical sailboat, and intelligence. Note: the Black Water Cult secretly takes arms and materiel via m'Thorsz-approved caravans (unknown to him) while planning to disrupt his trade routes — and runs a mole inside m'Thorsz's palace.
- Key figures: see *m'Thorsz*; allied with *Clan Gideon*.
- Status: active; patron faction.
- sources: The Dark Arts of Diplomacy: The Holy Cities.md; The Dark Arts: Raid the Cult's Desert Fort.md (and multiple other sources)

## Cultures & Peoples

**Tolorian peoples** (also *Tolorians, Vanicul speakers, Vanic citizens*) — The peoples of the Vanic Imperium, named after the famous early ruler Tolor and sharing the common language Vanicul.
- Relationship to the party/world: all player characters originate from Vanic states — this is the PC origin culture.
- Status: active.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

**Tugari** (also *Tugari Warriors, Tugari People*) — Nomadic warrior culture(s) of the Saragubi Desert, anchored to the Tugari Homeland. Descendants of or connected to the ancient Ukar civilization, holding an ancient pact with the Granite Empire.
- Character: renowned for exceptional horsemanship, desert survival, and combat prowess; use outriders on fast horses to avoid the giant worms. Female leadership structure (Tenetu is leader), amused by foreigners who don't understand their organizational system. Use ritual combat as a testing/negotiation method. Maintain trade relationships (Gamul trades weapons with them). Aware of but deliberately avoid the Charnel Keep region ("pointless and dangerous").
- Relationship to the party/world: first major encounter during the Basq adventure involved ritual-combat negotiation; granted the party passage and assigned Onsun as a minder/companion. Strongly opposed to the environmental changes caused by the Lillith Nomads' storm orchids — multiple violent interactions with the nomads, making them potentially hostile to the party's orchid-planting plans.
- Key figures: Tenetu (leader); Weshkhasi; Onsun; trade contact Gamul.
- Status: active, respected.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Journal; Basq - Kalinore's Aftermath.md; Kalinore's Chaos in the Saragubi Desert.md

**Lillith Nomads** (also *Lilith Nomads*) — Nomadic alliance of the deep desert / cloud region, keepers of ancient Bokkaran environmental magic, with druids trained as forest guardians.
- Goals: cultivate Bokkaran storm orchids (Alorix) to restore the desert to a lush state.
- Relationship to the party/world: allied faction. The party allied with them (Day 24), agreeing to plant storm orchid seeds in exchange for Bokkaran knowledge and plant samples; distribution sites include Great Oasis, Oasis of Sia, Tel A'bib, and possibly Chandler's Folly. Their orchid project puts them in direct conflict with the *Tugari*.
- Key figures: Bely Arys (leader).
- Status: active; party-allied.
- sources: Journal

**Ardoni nomads** (also *Ardoni*) — Nomadic people of the Hothme region and the Forge of Rhakk, with a seat on Hothme's Great Council. Also suspected of raiding merchants in the Forge of Rhakk.
- Status: active / unresolved.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

**B'rathian People** (also *B'rathians*) — Desert village community of B'rath, primarily children and families. All speak Ukar fluently (taught by Mesos). Their rite of passage involves harvesting fire beetle oil in caves and enduring the Bokkaran Dream Trap to reach adulthood (with some casualties). Receive supplies and knowledge from Mesos in exchange.
- Relationship to the party/world: friendly community and a potential recruitment source; the party gains village support and may recruit Ukar-fluent B'rathian youths as allies.
- Key figures: see *Mesos*.
- Status: friendly community.
- sources: The Titan's Finger and the Fire Beetles Adventure.md; Return to Mesos.md; Freeing Mesos.md

**Rrave Tribe (Gnolls)** — Gnoll tribe operating an Oasis in the Saragubi Desert, having earned the right to run it by killing the giant sand worm that guarded it. Described as "easy-going" and non-hostile.
- Relationship to the party/world: neutral/friendly. The party traded with them (Day 27), sold a storm orchid, and learned of local Saragubi politics and rival groups.
- Status: neutral/friendly.
- sources: Journal

**Titankin (Titans)** (also *Titan civilization, Titanic races*) — Ancient powerful beings, each born with a unique elemental trait (e.g., Mesos: earth/seismic sense), who at maturity infuse their essence into personal Khezurai rings (elemental bands). Their war with the Bokkaran ended in their dismemberment and imprisonment within magical Dream Catchers; multiple Titans remain scattered across the world in various prisons. They share a sacred bond with the Al-Faiz tree and seek to escape and reach Sefurah Springs / Al-Faiz.
- Relationship to the party/world: emerging ally faction. The party frees Mesos (the primary Titan encountered), learns Titan history and Khezurai ring mechanics, and plans to awaken Al-Faiz and reestablish the Titan–nature connection.
- Key figures: see *Mesos*.
- Status: emerging ally; legendary history / future adventure hook.
- sources: Freeing Mesos.md; Return to Mesos.md; The Titan's Finger and the Fire Beetles Adventure.md

### Ancient Civilizations (historical peoples)

**Bokkaran civilization** (also *Bokkarans, Ancient Bokkarans, Bokkaran culture*) — Ancient, sophisticated magical civilization whose ruins, artifacts, and magic remain throughout Ajiibwan (ruins, tombs, religious centers; the Sea of Souls regions; underground networks). Their magic spanned elementalism, artifice, temporal magic, and necromancy. They created the magical "engine" infrastructure of the world: portals (The Disk, arches), Whistling Towers, the Charnel Keep power plant, the Sea of Souls engine, the Kantaron tower, language/spell-crafting engines, Bokkaran Runes, spell scrolls, and constructs/guardians. Built the Dream Trap (originally a youth inoculation device, later repurposed as an imprisonment/torture tool) and the Dream Catchers, and used Al Faiz as a weapon. Fought great wars against the Giants/Titans, clerical orders, the Ukar, and others — the Titan war ended in the Titans' dismemberment and imprisonment. Worshipped gods including Tanil (wilderness) and Imroth (performance). Generals include Ilymna.
- Relationship to the party/world: foundational lore. Directly underpins the current magic system; the party encounters Bokkaran ruins, artifacts, runes, spirits, and architecture throughout, fights a Bokkaran Guardian, and uncovers the Sea of Souls excavation conspiracy. Final remnants preserve knowledge through artifacts and texts.
- Status: historical / extinct (artifacts and remnants active).
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Lore - Primary Source materials; Journal; Mesos' Dock.md; Return to Mesos.md; The Titan's Finger and the Fire Beetles Adventure.md; The Dark Arts of Diplomacy: The Holy Cities.md; all adventure documents

**Ukar** (also *Ukkar; Ancient Ukar Federation, Ukar Federation; Ukar civilization/culture; Ukar Ideology*) — Ancient, powerful civilization predating the Bokkaran, centered on the Saragubi Desert and extending into the Holy Cities (partly Sheloah), the Sea of Souls networks, and B'rath. Structured as a federation with alliance and military organization, and described as "an ideology, not a race" — it included all walks of life, with three thrones (titan, giant, humanoid). Bonded with Al Faiz (the world tree) and Sefurah Springs. Possessed advanced elemental and portal magic: the portal network uses the Ukar wreath (Roshanai) and crystal bird (Parvaiz) as portable access points, activated only by an Ukar spellcaster or an Afra's Spark-infused user. Made white-metal items and guardian creatures. Their relationship with the Bokkaran was "functional" until conflict arose — they stole storm orchids from the Bokkaran to destroy that civilization. The Ukar language survives, taught by Mesos to the B'rathians. Knowledge keeper: Mesos.
- Relationship to the party/world: the party learns Ukar lore through artifacts (Days 5, 13, 20–21+), recovers Ukar artifacts (including from the Black Water Cult HQ), studies portal mechanics, and plans to restore Al-Faiz via the Hymn of Harmony, recruiting Ukar-fluent B'rathians.
- Key figures: see *Mesos*; connected to the *Tugari* (descendants).
- Status: historical / partially active (artifacts and lore remain).
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Lore - Primary Source materials; Journal; The Dark Arts: Raid the Cult's Desert Fort.md; Return to Mesos.md; The Titan's Finger and the Fire Beetles Adventure.md; Mesos' Dock.md

## Cults & Antagonist Organizations

**Black Water Cult** (also *Black Water, Order of Black Water, Black Water Clan, BW Cult/Cultists, Blackwater Cult*; sometimes operating under a Church cover) — A secretive criminal/mystical order operating across Ajiibwan: the Holy Cities (HQ hidden beneath the underground network of Sheloah), Hothme, the Saragubi Desert (a compound and a desert fort), and political networks throughout the Vanic Imperium. Members are identified by obsidian serpent rings. They worship Elsrok and follow the "Great Design."
- Goals / operations: manufacture chaos and seize control through engineered crises — extortion and destabilization of trade routes, blackmail and corruption of government officials, artifact theft, kidnapping, well/water poisoning (e.g., Abarul), and sabotage. They excavate beneath the desert fort toward Sea of Souls access. Their knights serve as heavy cavalry on Bulette mounts. They take arms and materiel from m'Thorsz (via approved caravans, unknown to him) while secretly planning to wreck his trade and power base, and run a mole in his palace. They provide military support to Maghiel at Charnel Keep in exchange for her magical/undead-control training.
- Relationship to the party/world: the primary antagonist faction. The party has disrupted operations repeatedly — the Gala dak compound takeover; investigation, capture of cultists (including "Dil Logoin" and "Reg Tol Mahon"); a raid on the Cult HQ (stealing Ukar artifacts); caravan defense; and an assault on the desert fort. Partially defeated but not eliminated; ongoing threat through its allies.
- Key figures: Maghiel (hidden leader); high operatives Sayed Azhar (political influence), Soraya Fawaz (orphanage cover), Dr. Murtaza Ghanem (poisoner); the infiltrator "Mouse" in Hothme government; a Black Water priest. Linked to Elsrok.
- Status: active and dangerous; primary antagonist, partially defeated.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Lore - Primary Source materials; Navigating the Saragubi Desert.md; Basq - Kalinore's Aftermath.md; Kalinore's Chaos in the Saragubi Desert.md; The Dark Arts of Diplomacy: The Holy Cities.md; The Dark Arts: Caravan Rescue.md; The Dark Arts: Black Water heist.md; The Dark Arts: Raid the Cult's Desert Fort.md

## Guilds, Merchant Houses & Local Bodies

### Hothme Merchant Houses

**Clan Gideon (Blue Diamond)** (also *Gideon, Gideon Clan, House Gideon*) — Powerful dwarven merchant house/clan, seated at Hothme with a trading house in the Holy Cities (Sheloah district). Major investments in goldsmiths, gems, potions, firearms, and magic, with Gate commerce involving Dran and Ars Vaskul; specializes in desert trade and artifact acquisition, holding closely guarded, generations-deep maps of the Saragubi Desert.
- Relationship to the party/world: an early patron and long-term ally — commissioned the party to plant a storm orchid in the Saragubi Desert, supplied maps and resources, facilitated their residence (via household manager Ritabu Loklir), and contributed party member Rienas. In the Holy Cities, provides letters of introduction (used for access) and intelligence. Aligned with the Granite Empire.
- Key figures: Barthazai Gideon (clan leader/patriarch); Roshona Gideon (Holy Cities house head); loremaster Barazen; deep-desert trader Tormundgrin; household manager Ritabu Loklir; party member Rienas. Associated contact: Gifrendi.
- Status: active, influential.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Finding the Crypt of the Saragubi Desert.md; Basq - Kalinore's Aftermath.md; Kalinore's Chaos in the Saragubi Desert.md; The Dark Arts of Diplomacy: The Holy Cities.md; Mesos' Dock.md

**Cascabel (Golden Camel)** (also *Golden Camel Trading House*) — The richest merchant house in Hothme for two centuries. Runs caravans across the Red Plains, trading morenassa incense for gold, spices, gems, delicacies, silks, and teas. Known for aggressively protecting its foreign merchant relationships. Led by the ruthless patriarch Adric Cascabel.
- Relationship to the party/world: engaged in trade negotiations across factions, including with Maghiel at the Sacred Pool (interrupted by her attack on the party). Rivals the rising Vedek house in the northern incense trade.
- Key figures: Adric Cascabel (patriarch); seat on the Hothme Common Council.
- Status: active.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Kalinore's Tomb adventure.md

**Vedek (Red Horse)** — Rising merchant house that made its fortune in diatryma and horse markets; trades exotic animals and animal products. Recently challenged Cascabel in the northern incense trade but suffered centaur-bandit attacks, with a whole caravan wiped out.
- Key figures: seat on the Hothme Common Council; rival of Cascabel.
- Status: active / threatened.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

**Zingolfin (Golden Moth)** — Merchant house with extensive interests in the cloth and silk trade, especially with Kel and Ryuvatar.
- Key figures: seat on the Hothme Common Council.
- Status: active.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

### Hothme Guilds & Underworld

**Scholar's Guild** (also *Scholars Guild*) — Educational and research organization in Hothme, run by a committee/board of scholars; each scholar casts one magical vote via a mage hand spell, and membership is secret even for lower ranks. Contains two member guilds — the Mechanicians' Guild (at Sylia Hall) and the Casting Guild (at Valeska's Needle) — plus the Hall of Antiquities, a non-casting group cataloging historical artifacts and ephemera.
- Status: active.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

**Casting Guild** — Member guild of the Scholar's Guild focused on magic casting, located at Valeska's Needle; also maintains portal access in the Grand Forum (the party's arrival point in the city). Appears to be a neutral organization. Led by the elf wizard Dolasea (arrogant, difficult, proud).
- Relationship to the party/world: a travel/arrival hub for the party; caught up in the m'Thorsz investigation of the Sacred Pool (Maghiel) incident.
- Key figures: Dolasea (leader); Burga (senior researcher — thoughtful, studious, timid); Irrissee (half-elf fighting warlock).
- Status: active.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands; Kalinore's Tomb adventure.md

**Mechanicians' Guild** — Member guild of the Scholar's Guild focused on mechanical crafts, located in Sylia Hall.
- Status: active.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

**Qual Sh'tar** — Secretive thieves guild operating in Hothme, gathering at the Crimson Shield Tavern. Trades in narcotics, loan sharking, and protection rackets.
- Key figures: Y'shasvak and Tajakof (leaders).
- Status: active.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

### Holy Cities & Local Bodies

**Hothme Great Council / Common Council** — The governing bodies of Hothme. The Great Council includes representation for the Ardoni nomads; the Common Council seats the major merchant houses (Cascabel, Gideon, Vedek, Zingolfin).
- Status: active.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

**Desert Scouts (Holy Cities militia)** (also *Scout Militia, Holy Cities Scout Forces*) — Military scout forces of the Holy Cities, skilled in desert warfare, tracking, and reconnaissance; maintain checkpoints and patrols. Led by Na'ilah Kopak.
- Relationship to the party/world: a recruitable military ally — fought alongside the party during the caravan defense and the desert fort assault, with negotiations to keep a scout presence at the captured fort.
- Key figures: Na'ilah Kopak.
- Status: military ally.
- sources: The Dark Arts: Caravan Rescue.md; The Dark Arts: Raid the Cult's Desert Fort.md

**Taliphan Militia** — Local militia defending Tel A'bib and surrounding areas.
- Relationship to the party/world: the party found dead Taliphan soldiers (Day 3, near the Oasis of Sia) and looted their equipment; members presumably served with Elmingwed during the Black Water Cult assault.
- Key figures: see *Elmingwed*.
- Status: active.
- sources: Journal

### Religious Bodies

**The Church** (also *Holy Church*) — Hierarchical religious organization (Cardinals, Deacons) of the Holy Cities and Hothme, maintaining the Church Campus. Hosted Maghiel for magical instruction; may overlap with or be infiltrated by the Black Water Cult.
- Key figures: Cardinal Sponfz; Deacons Milgrew and Polrf; see *Maghiel*.
- Status: active.
- sources: Lore - Primary Source materials

**Tanil Followers** (also *Followers of Tanil, Tanil Cult; Tanil Warlocks*) — Followers of Tanil, god of wilderness/nature, found throughout Ajiibwan (e.g., the Glade of Tanil, represented by dryads; Tanil Warlocks active around Tel A'bib responding to the construct plague). Tenets: prevent human encroachment on nature, do good, protect vulnerable travelers. In Tanil sanctuaries they can access prayer powers (healing, raise dead). Connected to the Arch-fey Pollana.
- Relationship to the party/world: a beneficial, party-aligned faction. The party encountered them (Day 26) and several members converted (A''ki, Kai, Silverin, and later others), with mechanical effects — Kai lost the ability to use the Ukkar White Death Hammer; A''ki gained a Second Sight blessing. Members receive boons from buried undead-creature skeletons (Elk, Lion, Bear, Wolf from Kalinore's cavern); their concern for nature motivates action against Duroigh.
- Key figures: Tanil (deity); Pollana (Arch-fey); see *Dimark*.
- Status: active; beneficial to / aligned with the party.
- sources: Journal; Escaping the Crypt of the Saragubi Desert.md; Crisis at Kantaron Summit.md

**Athyes religion** (also *Athyes faith, Athyism*) — Religious faith practiced in Tel A'bib, with a temple overseen by senior priests.
- Key figures: Ennett Wete; Sybel Ceray.
- Status: active.
- sources: Lore - Ajiibwan Wilderlands

## Who Opposes Whom — Relationship Summary

- **Black Water Cult vs. nearly everyone:** the cult covertly preys on the *Granite Empire* (stealing m'Thorsz's materiel, planning to wreck his trade, running a palace mole), infiltrates and destabilizes the *Vanic Imperium* and the *Church*, and is the party's primary antagonist.
- **Granite Empire ↔ party (allied):** m'Thorsz and *Clan Gideon* patronize and supply the party; the Granite Empire also works to prop up the *Vanic Imperium* against collapse.
- **Lillith Nomads vs. Tugari:** the nomads' storm-orchid project to re-green the desert directly threatens the traditionalist *Tugari*, producing repeated violent clashes — and putting the party (allied with the nomads) on a collision course with the Tugari.
- **Cascabel vs. Vedek:** rival Hothme merchant houses fighting over the northern incense trade; Vedek's caravan was wiped out by centaur bandits.
- **Ancient Ukar vs. Bokkaran:** a once-"functional" relationship that turned to war — the Ukar stole storm orchids to destroy the Bokkaran; the Bokkaran in turn warred with and imprisoned the *Titans*. These ancient conflicts still shape the world's magic and ruins.
- **Tanil Followers vs. encroachment/Duroigh:** nature-protecting faction aligned with the party, opposed to destruction of the wild (and to Duroigh).
- **Vanic Imperium — passive/bureaucratic:** not an active enemy, but its indifference (e.g., to Kantaron Summit and Basq) repeatedly forces *m'Thorsz* / the party to step in.
