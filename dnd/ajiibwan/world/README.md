# Ajiibwan World Compendium

The organized, cross-referenced catalog of **everything in the Deserts of Ajiibwan** —
synthesized from the 51 converted homebrew Google Docs (see [`../assets/`](../assets/))
and the play history. Built to answer "what/who/where is this, and what happened with it"
fast, so new adventures can reuse five years of established world.

> ## ⏮ This is a RESET playthrough — read play-history as *reference*
> The new campaign rewinds the sandbox to the **exact state the first party started in**.
> Everything the prior party did is **reference only** — plot hooks, precedent, and
> reusable scenarios — **not** inherited history. The new world starts **pre-trigger**:
> the Black Water Cult is at full strength, the Kantaron demon was never summoned,
> Afra/Mesos are dormant, Maghiel's plot hasn't begun, sites are unlooted, and the prior
> PCs don't exist in this timeline.
>
> So the `play_history` and `status` fields below describe **the prior playthrough**, not
> the new game's current state. Treat them as "here's how it went / what's possible," and
> reset the live state when seeding `../campaign/state.yaml`.
>
> **Design north star — "RPG ouija board":** the world goes where the party steers, but it
> *doesn't care* about the party and advances on its own. Design for an autonomous living
> world, not a party-centric rail. In-world time runs **faster than real time** this run.

| [`gazetteer.md`](gazetteer.md) | **Places** — every region, sited location (with adventure #, level, hex coord), and sublocation; what's there and what the prior party did. Ends with a master table of all sited sites. |
| [`adventures.md`](adventures.md) | **Adventure catalog** — every homebrew adventure (premise, structure, prior outcome, source doc), plus the staged "unplaced/candidate" modules and the sited-but-unwritten (PDF-only) sites. |

| [`factions.md`](factions.md) | **Factions & peoples** — powers, cultures, cults, guilds and merchant houses, plus a "who opposes whom" summary. |
| [`artifacts.md`](artifacts.md) | **Artifacts & items** — major/plot artifacts, magic items, custom Bokkaran infusions, currency, gear; quick reference of what the party holds vs. what's unrecovered. |

## How it was built & how to trust it

Generated by reading the full converted corpus (`assets/_raw/*.md`) with parallel
extractors, then merging/deduplicating per category. It is a **strong first synthesis,
not hand-verified canon.** Known caveats, flagged for Steve's review:

- **Source conflicts are preserved, not resolved** — e.g. whether the Bokkaran's ancient
  enemy was the *Ukar Federation*, the *Titans/Titankin*, or a once-unified culture; and
  the identity of "the Slumbering Titan" (Afra in the Granite Empire ruins vs. the
  imprisoned **Mesos** beneath B'rath). Treat these as open questions.
- **Spelling variants** appear as the docs use them (Farrav'n/Favvar'n, Doorne/Dorne,
  Ukar/Ukkar, Maghiel/Magdaliel, Kantaron/Kantaroz, Lilth/Lillith). Not normalized.
- **Possible name collisions** noted inline (e.g. multiple "Jiyloo", "Abet").
- `sources:` cite the homebrew doc filenames; once `tools/convert.py` runs, these can be
  rewritten as links to the committed `assets/` slugs.

Corrections are easy and welcome — these are plain markdown. The structure (regions,
entity types, cross-links) is the durable part; the contents will sharpen with your edits.

**Start here for review:** [`../analysis/open-questions.md`](../analysis/open-questions.md)
collects the conflicts, duplications, and ambiguities found during the build, as inline
questions to answer. Once answered, the resolutions get propagated back into these files.

## Conventions

- **Cross-links** use slugs: `[[loc:hothme]]`, `[[npc:maghiel]]`, `[[faction:black-water-cult]]`,
  `[[item:clasp-of-vergen]]`. These resolve to entries in `entities.yaml` / the compendium.
- **Hex coords + adventure index** map to the GM's **Roll20** hex map (maps live in Roll20,
  not the repo — see [`../../CLAUDE.md`](../../CLAUDE.md)).
