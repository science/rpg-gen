# Adventure Catalog — Deserts of Ajiibwan

GM-facing index of every adventure in the open-world Ajiibwan sandbox. The campaign is a
sandbox: this catalog groups adventures by region, but **the order is soft** — the party
chooses where to go, and most arcs can be entered, paused, or skipped. Each entry links
back to its converted source under `assets/_raw/...`. "Prior campaign" outcomes are the
*previous* party's play history; where the input recorded none, the entry says so — these
are not invented.

---

## Written Adventures (homebrew)

### Hothme & the Granite Empire

**Exploring Hothme and the Granite Empire** — Hothme city, underground port, Clan Gideon compound
- premise: After early trading success, the party arrives at Hothme, fighting off Sea of Souls creatures at the underground port. They meet Clan Gideon dwarves (Grinav, Khali), explore markets, and negotiate housing and business to build a network for future Granite Empire access.
- structure: Underground port defense; Clan Gideon meeting; city exploration; merchant negotiations; wine-and-dine scenarios.
- prior campaign: Established a base in Hothme; secured relationship with Clan Gideon; negotiated housing rates and trade benefits; began building city reputation.
- source: `assets/_raw/Hothme (x) 0117/Exploring Hothme and the Granite Empire.md`

**Returning to Hothme from the Saragubi** — Sea of Souls route to Hothme, Hothme port, Clan Gideon compound
- premise: The party returns to Hothme by Sea of Souls boat after desert adventures; the boat is damaged and must be repaired. A massive scorpion horde attacks in transit. On arrival they meet Clan Gideon leadership, reclaim stored property, and arrange a permanent lease.
- structure: Scorpion horde combat (area-effect emphasis); port arrival; Clan Gideon negotiation; social wine-and-dine event with skill-based activities.
- prior campaign: Defeated three scorpion hordes (45 total); secured a permanent lease with Clan Gideon; negotiated favorable housing/lab/stable rates; gained intelligence and reputation.
- source: `assets/_raw/Hothme (x) 0117/Returning to Hothme from the Saragubi.md`

**Valeska's Needle, Afra's Temple, and the Scholar's Guild** — Valeska's Needle (Hothme), Grand Forum Pools, Sacred Baths
- premise: The party emerges from Afra's Temple portal inside wizard Burga's private library in Valeska's Needle, startling the Scholar's Guild. Burga trades library access for information and artifacts. The party discovers Kalinore's music box, learns of his tomb in the Saragubi, and meets Casting Guild head Dolasea at the Grand Forum Pools.
- structure: Burga's Library encounter and negotiation; music box discovery; rock-skipping blessing game at Grand Forum Pools; Maghiel sighting plotting with merchant Adric Cascabel; combat at the Sacred Baths (assassins Gotrin and Tarlyn).
- prior campaign: Negotiated library access and portal use; discovered Kalinore's music box and learned the tomb location; gained rock-skipping blessings; engaged Maghiel in combat (she escaped via Dimension Door); uncovered merchant ties to her plans.
- source: `assets/_raw/Hothme (x) 0117/Valeska's Needle, Afra's Temple, and the Scholar's Guild.md`

### Sarklan Desert (Ukar Ruins & Nurian Necropolis)

**Slumbering Titan Adventure** — Sarklan Desert, Ukar Ruins (80 miles west of Hothme)
- premise: Clan Gideon supplies a Deactivate Ukar Rune scroll to unlock a sealed Ukar ruin. The party explores titanic architecture, fights ancient guardians and a slumbering Spinosaurus, and awakens Afra — a Titan architect of the Great Web of portals — learning of the Bokkaran-Ukar war and the portal network.
- structure: Wayfinding; unlocking the ruins; fighting Degenerate Titans and Spinosaurus; exploring Afra's Tower/Temple/Palace; recovering Afra's Spark; repairing the Mytholabe Ninla; awakening Afra; activating the portal network; returning through portal to Valeska's Needle.
- prior campaign: Awakened Afra; gained Afra's Spark and portal knowledge; encountered the Mytholabe Ninla; returned to Hothme via the Valeska's Needle portal, surprising the Scholar's Guild.
- source: `assets/_raw/Granite Empire/Slumbering Titan Adventure.md`

**The Tugari in Need** — Nurian Necropolis (Granite Empire, located via Bokkaran documents)
- premise: Onsun Tugari asks the party to rescue kidnapped tribespeople. They decipher Bokkaran documents to find the Necropolis, cross the desert (goblins, ancient battlefields), explore its chambers, and confront Maghiel's necromantic earth-draining rituals.
- structure: Desert travel; Ironbound Nomads / Salajah Al-Sahara scorpion encounter; Echoing Ruins of Battle; Necropolis exploration (Guardians, Chamber of Whispers, Garden of Stone, Archives, Observatory, Crypt of Shadows); Observatory ley-line manipulation; Drow patrols, Embalmer constructs, undead, Dark Spirits.
- prior campaign: Rescued Tugari from a petrification curse; located the ley-line lens; fought a Dark Spirit and Revenant; prepared to descend into deeper Crypt chambers.
- source: `assets/_raw/Granite Empire/The Tugari in Need/The Tugari in Need.md`

### Sefurah Springs / Al Faiz (the Maghiel climax arc)

**In Search of Al Faiz (Sefurah Springs)** — Elden village, Blackwater Swamp, Sefurah Springs
- premise: After chasing off Elden warriors, the party parleys with villagers enslaved by the water demon Ylgavroth. To win freedom and passage, they must recover the weapon Blackwater's Dirge from the swamp and defeat the demon.
- structure: Spy on the village meeting; parley; seek Blackwater's Dirge in the swamp (Swamp Elemental); defeat Ylgavroth; gain a navigation blessing and map to Al Faiz.
- prior campaign: Defeated the Waterfall Demon Ylgavroth; obtained Blackwater's Dirge and a blessing/guidance to Al Faiz; acquired several magical artifacts (Prismatic Translocator, Gale-Forged Armor, Torrential Gauntlets, and more).
- source: `assets/_raw/Granite Empire/Al Faiz and Sefurah Springs/In Search of Al Faiz (Sefurah Springs).md`

**Twin Altars (Sefurah Springs)** — Bokkaran Air Altar (mountain summit) & Ukar Earth Altar (arena)
- premise: The Elden guide the party to twin Air and Earth altars whose blessings unlock the inner gardens of Al Faiz. They cross drained Sea of Souls caverns, confront elemental and undead guardians, and earn the favor of the goddess Bokkara and the Ukar.
- structure: Crystal Grove / Whispering Hall / Echo Chamber exploration; Air Altar trials and Bokkara's revelation; descent to the Earth Altar arena; Ukar Earth Titan stone-golem fight; freeing the Ukar champion Balaquou; encounters with Tempestarian, Revenant, Lak Sothoth, Zephyrbane Phantasm.
- prior campaign: Met the goddess Bokkara and received individual blessings (Resonant Accord, Gale's Insight, Stalwart's Endurance, Warden's Roar, Luminous Duality, Ingenious Bombardier); defeated the Tempestarian elementals and the Ukar Earth Titan; gained Staff of the Sacred Ward; readied to confront Maghiel.
- source: `assets/_raw/Granite Empire/Al Faiz and Sefurah Springs/Twin Altars (Sefurah Springs).md`

**Battle for Sefura** — Sefura Gardens, approach to Al Faiz (level 16-20)
- premise: A large-scale mass-combat encounter as the party and allies break through Maghiel's army of undead and living soldiers before reaching Sefura Gardens. Resolved through battlefield zones and skill checks rather than turn-by-turn combat.
- structure: Multiple battlefield zones (Frontline Clash, Undead Siege, Arcane Storm, Healer's Sanctum, Flanking Maneuver, Supply Caravan) with horde mechanics and a strategic breakthrough.
- prior campaign: Weakened Maghiel's forces enough to breach Sefura Gardens; left allies behind and proceeded into the gardens with two companions for the final confrontation.
- source: `assets/_raw/Granite Empire/Al Faiz and Sefurah Springs/Battle to Enter Sefurah/Battle for Sefura.md`

**Final Confrontation in Al Faiz** — Al Faiz temple compound, Sefurah Gardens (level 16-20+)
- premise: The party infiltrates Maghiel's ritual chamber, where a ritual imprisoning a cleric advances each round. They discover Maghiel is corrupted by the Black Thorn and must choose her fate: remove the Thorn (risking her death), leave it (pragmatic, ambiguous), or destroy both.
- structure: Infiltration; ritual interruption; Jiyloo's sacrifice (loses her hand saving the cleric); Maghiel boss fight; the Black Thorn moral choice; landscape rebirth and political/cultural epilogue.
- prior campaign: Defeated/subdued Maghiel; made the Black Thorn choice (outcome varies by player decision); witnessed the desert transform from wasteland to fertile land; triggered cascading political, cultural, and magical consequences across Ajiibwan.
- source: `assets/_raw/Granite Empire/Al Faiz and Sefurah Springs/Final Conflict/Final Confrontation in Al Faiz.md`

**Radiant Necropolis (Chandler's Folly)** — Thari (stone giant) temple / Ukar palace, Sea of Souls (level 4-6)
- premise: A Thari (stone giant) temple — allies of the Ukar — guards a stretch of the Sea of Souls where sacrificed blood is used to neutralize the "unseen water." The party must clear the stone guardians to open the northern passage toward Bokkaral, exploring the temple, an Ukar Royal Throne Room, and the descent to an abandoned Ukar village, mausoleum, and Queen's Tomb.
- structure: Numbered temple rooms (#0 cave-in entrance through #8): ghoul-guarded entry, hidden sacrifice room, stone-creature passages, a walled-in insane undead Bokkaran wizard (ring of ward of spellcasting), the major-sacrifice drain room and Stone Guardian fight (Ukar White Death Hammer, Stoneskin scroll), the giant Royal Throne Room (learn Ukar by surviving the throne's psychic test); deep descent to the 500'-down Ukar village, the Ukar Mausoleum (Whu-minh, venomous mummy), and the Ukar Queen's Tomb (Imperial Ghoul, Darakhuls; an embedded green gem grants an Ukar sorcerer spell slot).
- prior campaign: not recorded / not yet run.
- source: `assets/_raw/Chandler's Folly - Radiant Necropolis (Level 4-6) 2510/Radiant Necropolis dungeon notes.md`

**Dimark Village & Tanil's Glade** — Dimark village; Tanil's Glade, the smoky region north-west of Tel A'bib (level 3-4)
- premise: A rescued villager (Sirende) tells of Dimark and a magical spell engine in Tanil's Glade. The party can earn the villagers' trust, reach the Glade past its Fomorian guardian, and gain spellcasting from Tanil — by pledging service, by force (incurring a curse), or by freeing the bound faeries (angering Tanil).
- structure: Win the villagers' trust (Nabi, Samulu, Goni, Ninla); reach Tanil's Glade past the Fomorian giant and dire wolf; the Bokkaran temple and eternal glade with soul-stealing dryads; choose how to gain Tanil's magic — embrace Tanil as deity (a magical compulsion removable only by remove curse), fight the faeries (cursed engine use), or destroy the altar to free the faeries.
- prior campaign: not recorded / not yet run.
- source: `assets/_raw/Dimark (Level 3-4) (1210)/Dimark Village and Adventure.md`

**Air Spire** — The Air Spire, western side of the Great Oasis
- premise: A stone tower on the landside of the barren western Great Oasis provides gondola access up to the Air Spire (a floating sphere). Wyverns nest in the fallen-in tower top, ghouls guard the entrance, and dormant heavy-machinery constructs wait in the machine room.
- structure: Keyed tower rooms: ghoul-guarded entrance (1); waiting room linking a dining room and servant's room (2); control room (5) and machine room with dormant repair constructs that attack if disturbed; gondola access to the Air Spire (4); a former living/sleeping area now a wyvern lair (6). (Sketch/skeleton notes only.)
- prior campaign: not recorded / not yet run.
- source: `assets/_raw/Air Spire (2)/Air Spire adventure.md`
